Final Brief Pack

How to use this pack

This is the one-stop handover index for the Design phase's nine briefs — six channel briefs (SEO, Paid Media, EDM, CRM, PR, Events) and three creative briefs (Design, Website, Photo/Video). Each brief is written to be handed to a freelancer, agency, in-house marketer or Maxxim action skill without the underlying plan open beside it. Read this index first to see what exists, what depends on what, and where to start; then open only the specific brief relevant to the work at hand.

The common thread

Every brief in this pack tells the same story, drawn from positioning.html: for car sellers aged 60–75+ across the Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers who want relief rather than a negotiation, Adam Hall Buy My Car is the trust-and-ease-maximising, fully personal way to sell a car — the one where the same known person handles the whole thing, at the seller's home, with a number meant to hold. The proof behind that promise is confirmed: 27+ years in the automotive trade, and 10,000+ live, on-air valuations delivered since 2010 as host of "What's Your Car Worth?" on 4CRB 89.3FM. No researched competitor is a person — CarBuyers and We Buy Cars are companies delivered through branches or panels, and carsales Instant Offer routes to a dealer network, not to carsales itself.

Ten different operators picking up nine different briefs cannot fragment this brand if each one holds three things constant. The voice is plain, unhurried and never corporate (copy-deck.html). The offer is a free, obligation-free, explained number, never a discount or an urgency push. And no operator states as fact anything not yet confirmed by Adam: the "one number, never moved" guarantee is excluded from every public-facing claim in this pack pending CQ01 to CQ04, and CQ11 (whether the brand permanently carries Adam's personal name) is treated neutrally throughout — every brief is written to hold under the current name.

Sequencing

Nothing in this pack is a demand-generation push at sign-off. Campaign 1, "Prove the Engine" (campaign-calendar.html, months 1–3), is instrumentation and asset-formalisation only — crm-brief.html's lead log going live is the precondition every other brief's reporting depends on, and no paid spend runs under either budget scenario during this window.

design-brief.html, website-brief.html and photo-video-brief.html can start immediately and in parallel — they do not depend on the CRM lead log or on Adam's outstanding CORE questions, provided each is built to hold under the current brand name. seo-brief.html's technical foundation rides directly on the website build. edm-brief.html's automation and paid-media-brief.html's landing pages both gate behind the website shipping analytics, call tracking and a callback form — neither should be activated against the current five-page brochure site. pr-brief.html holds all outreach until written permission for the 10,000+ valuations claim and testimonials is secured. events-brief.html runs groundwork only in Quarter 1, with its first community talk in Quarter 2.

Paid media specifically activates only once all three Now-to-Next triggers hold together (paid-media-brief.html): at least one full month of usable lead-log data, CQ11/CQ06/CQ08 answered, and a second-buyer conversation under way. A date alone is not the trigger — the conditions are.

Gaps

No brief in this pack is missing its plan — all nine source documents (seo-strategy.html, paid-media-plan.html, edm-plan.html, crm-plan.html, pr-plan.html, events-plan.html, and the three creative briefs' own foundation work) exist and are approved. The genuine gaps sit inside the plans themselves, not between them: no keyword-volume, rank-tracking or backlink-audit tool exists yet for SEO; no paid-media performance history exists on any platform to set a target CPA against; no subscriber list or CRM system exists to build EDM or lead-log automation from real data; no PR track record or media relationship exists beyond the organic 4CRB partnership; and no event has yet run to set a realistic attendance or conversion assumption. Every brief states these gaps openly rather than inventing a figure to fill them, and every projected date above should be treated as a working plan, not a guarantee, until the lead log itself confirms it.
