Website Brief

1. Project overview

Project type. A full rebuild, not a redesign of the existing pages in place. The current site, adamhallbuymycar.com.au, is a five-page WordPress/Elementor brochure — Home, How it Works, Buy My Car, Adam's Story, Listen, Contact — with the phone number 0404 290 617 as the only call to action, no analytics, no schema markup, and no content built for the estate/health-crisis segment or for Northern Rivers specifically (website-crawl-digest.html). This brief scopes its replacement: a 15-page, phone-first, accessibility-led site built to convert radio listeners, referrals, cold search visitors and AI-assistant visitors alike into a phone call or a logged callback request.

Business objectives this site supports. Full detail in website-strategy.html; in summary — convert first exposure into a call without ever pressuring the visitor; be legible to a 60–75+ audience and to an adult child researching on a parent's behalf; formalise the radio and testimonial proof into permanent on-site assets; give the estate/health-crisis segment and Northern Rivers a dedicated home neither has today; and instrument the site from day one, since it is currently the only completely unmeasured part of the business (measurement-plan.html).

Target launch date. Not yet fixed by the client; build should proceed now regardless, since this work does not depend on Adam's 22 outstanding CORE questions or on the CQ11 brand-naming decision (website-strategy.html, Phase recommendation) — provided the site is built to work fully under the current name and would not need a structural rebuild if CQ11 later resolves differently.

Key problems this rebuild solves. No analytics or call tracking exists today, so nothing about channel effectiveness can be judged with evidence (conversion-pathway.html); no content exists for the two open-gap opportunities (estate/health-crisis sellers, Northern Rivers coverage); no schema markup or AI-visibility structuring exists, despite a confirmed rise in "how do I sell my father's car"-style AI-assistant queries (seo-strategy.html, section 9); and the current site was not built with this audience's accessibility needs foregrounded — body text sizing, tap targets and form simplicity all need deliberate attention that a stock Elementor build did not receive.

Scope boundary, stated explicitly. This brief covers the marketing/brochure site only. The e-commerce/retail marketplace confirmed as a separate, later-priced Phase 2 product line in business-plan.html has zero presence in this build — it is a different product for a later stage, not a hidden section of this site (sitemap.html).

4. Functional requirements

Forms — the callback-request form (Contact page and embedded on Sell Your Car hub, the three regional pages, and Home). Five fields, no more: name; phone number; suburb; best time to call; an optional free-text "tell us about your car" field. Validation: light only — required-field checks on name and phone, a simple phone-format check, no strict pattern-matching that could reject a valid but unusually formatted number. Submission destination: the manual lead log recommended in measurement-plan.html, plus an email notification to Adam; no CRM integration at launch (a later Deploy-phase decision once volume is proven, per website-strategy.html). Confirmation behaviour: an on-page thank-you message stating someone will call back, plus an optional confirmation email if an email field is later added — not required at launch since email is not one of the five fields. Spam protection: honeypot field or equivalent invisible technique only — explicitly no CAPTCHA, flagged as an accessibility risk for this audience in website-strategy.html's conversion strategy.

Search. Not applicable — a 15-page flat site has no content volume to justify on-site search, and adding one would be an unnecessary interface element for this audience.

Filtering/sorting. Not applicable — no product catalogue or listings exist on this site.

User accounts. Not applicable — no login, registration or profile functionality anywhere on this site.

E-commerce. Not applicable — explicitly out of scope, confirmed separate Phase 2 product line (business-plan.html, sitemap.html).

Bookings/calendars. Not applicable at launch. A home-visit "booking" is conducted entirely by phone once a caller connects with Adam — there is deliberately no online booking widget, since scheduling a visit is part of the personal conversation, not a self-service step.

Calculators/tools. Explicitly excluded. No online instant-valuation or quote calculator — a valuation is a home-visit human judgement, and the underlying rules remain HIGH-CONTROL pending Adam's confirmation (CQ01–CQ03). Building a self-service calculator would misrepresent the model and contradict the brand's "no games" tone.

Maps. A simple, static service-area indicator (a labelled map or a plain list of the three regions/named towns) on the Contact page and the three regional pages — no interactive Google Maps embed with pin-drop functionality is required, since there is no fixed premises to navigate to; keep this lightweight and load-cost-free.

Live chat. Not recommended at launch. This audience's stated and confirmed channel preference is phone over any digital-first contact method (customer-profile.html); live chat would add a second, lower-preference contact surface without solving a problem the phone number and callback form don't already solve, and risks feeling impersonal for the estate/health-crisis segment specifically.

tel: links — the site's primary functional requirement. Every instance of the phone number, site-wide, must be a working tel:0404290617 link, not plain text — in the persistent header, the sticky mobile footer button, every page's close/CTA block, and the footer NAP. This is the single most important functional element on the entire site, matching the phone-first conversion strategy in website-strategy.html.

5. Design requirements

Brand guidelines reference. brand-guidelines.html for palette, type and imagery direction; logo-brief.html for the mark, once refined. Both are explicitly provisional — the site must be built so a settled logo and any confirmed hex adjustment can be dropped in without a structural rebuild.

Responsive breakpoints. Mobile: up to 599px (primary design target — the sticky call button is a mobile-first pattern, not a desktop feature ported down, per website-strategy.html). Tablet: 600–1023px. Desktop: 1024px and above. Design mobile-first throughout, given the expectation that most enquiries arrive by phone in hand.

Accessibility standards. WCAG 2.1 AA as the floor, with deliberate choices beyond AA for this specific audience: minimum 18px body text (matching brand-guidelines.html's Body Large/Body type-scale rows); strong colour contrast (verify every text/background pairing from the provisional palette meets at least 4.5:1, particularly Brass Trust on Paper); large tap targets (48px minimum) for the phone and callback CTAs; no auto-advancing carousels anywhere on the site; no CAPTCHA; single-step forms only; no interaction pattern that assumes a confident, mouse-fluent visitor (no hover-triggered menus, no accordion-only content that hides essential information behind a click). Test the "find the number and call" and "submit a callback request" tasks with an older test-user cohort before launch, per website-strategy.html Objective 2 — do not assume this works from design intent alone.

Animation and interaction preferences. Subtle only — a gentle fade-in on scroll is acceptable; no parallax, no auto-playing video with sound, no scroll-jacking. This audience's stated preference and this brand's "calm, unhurried" tone both argue against anything that feels flashy or attention-grabbing for its own sake.

Design system approach. Component-based — a shared header, footer, CTA block, testimonial card, FAQ accordion (each individual question expandable, but never hiding the question text itself) and hero pattern reused across all 15 pages, so the three regional pages and any future page share a consistent, quick-to-build structure rather than one-off page designs.

6. Technical requirements

CMS platform. A static site build (Maxxim's Astro-based build chain, per website-strategy.html's recommendation), not a rebuild of the current WordPress/Elementor site in place. Rationale: the current site has no analytics, no structured schema, no per-suburb pages and no AI-visibility structuring — patching WordPress to add all of this is more effort than a rebuild that ships these by default, and a static output directly serves the Core Web Vitals and accessibility targets below.

Hosting. Cloudflare Pages, via Maxxim's web-deploy skill (preview environment, then promote to production) — low-cost, high-performance static hosting appropriate for expected traffic levels (a single-operator local service business, not a high-volume e-commerce site).

SSL. Cloudflare-managed SSL certificate, issued automatically as part of the hosting setup — no separate certificate purchase required.

Domain. Primary domain: adamhallbuymycar.com.au (the existing, brand-recognised domain — carry it forward rather than launching on a new domain, since it already carries whatever brand-search equity exists). No subdomains required. Domain registrar and DNS changeover process per Maxxim's domain-setup skill.

CDN. Included by default via Cloudflare Pages; imagery served from assets.maxxim.ai (see Producing the assets, below) is separately CDN-distributed.

Page speed targets. Core Web Vitals green across all three metrics — LCP under 2.5s, INP under 200ms, CLS under 0.1 — achievable by default on a static rebuild with optimised images and no render-blocking scripts, per website-strategy.html's technical-requirements section.

Integrations. Call tracking (method: a tracked, source-attributing phone number or dynamic number insertion, so phone conversions are attributable to source — closing the single biggest measurement gap in measurement-plan.html); web analytics (method: script embed); Google Search Console (method: DNS or meta-tag verification); Google Business Profile (method: manual claim/verify, aligned to the single NAP source in the footer). No CRM integration at launch — the manual lead log in measurement-plan.html is the launch-scope destination for callback-form submissions; a CRM is a later Deploy-phase decision once volume from the site is proven.

7. SEO requirements

Full keyword targeting and content strategy live in seo-strategy.html; this section is the technical plumbing this build must ship with, per that document's Layer-1 standard.

URL structure and naming conventions. Flat, human-readable, lower-case, hyphenated slugs, matching section 2's table exactly — for example /sell-your-car/northern-rivers, /family-car-sale. No query strings, no dates or IDs in URLs, no deep nesting beyond the one-level regional pages.

Page title and meta description approach. Title formula: primary local or intent keyword, then a short service descriptor, then the brand name (aim for 60 characters or fewer) — for example "Sell My Car Northern Rivers | Free Home-Visit Valuation | Adam Hall Buy My Car." Meta description formula: core promise, geography, a soft call to action (aim for 155 characters or fewer). Full worked examples in seo-strategy.html, section 5.

Schema markup requirements. LocalBusiness JSON-LD site-wide (name, address if applicable, phone, hours, service area, services) as the single highest-impact fix, given zero existing structuring; Service schema on the Sell Your Car hub and the three regional pages; FAQPage schema on the FAQ page; Review schema explicitly deferred until a genuine public review platform exists — never fabricated ahead of that, per seo-strategy.html's technical-audit framework.

Redirects from the old site — 301 redirect map. The current five-page WordPress site presumably holds whatever organic and brand-search equity exists today (27-years, "Adam Hall" name searches); every current URL must 301 to its new-site equivalent before the old site is retired, or that equity is lost outright (seo-strategy.html, technical-audit priority 2). 8. Content management

Who updates content post-launch. Both. Maxxim/agency drafts and maintains structural content (new regional pages, schema, technical SEO) per the cadence in content-strategy.html; Adam or a future support hire adds new testimonials and reviews to the Testimonials page as they're collected, and confirms any HIGH-CONTROL-adjacent copy before it's published (valuation claims, settlement-process detail).

CMS training requirements. One session, covering: adding a new testimonial entry; updating the FAQ; adding a What's Your Car Worth? recap entry. Kept intentionally minimal, since this is a static-site build with a lightweight content model, not a full CMS with a complex editorial workflow.

Content workflow. Draft (Maxxim/agency or Adam) → review (Adam, specifically for anything touching a valuation claim, a testimonial's exact wording, or the estate/health-crisis content) → publish. No content that touches a HIGH-CONTROL-adjacent claim (valuation rules, settlement mechanics, an unconditional guarantee) should publish without Adam's direct confirmation, per the flag carried through every upstream Design-phase document.

Editorial permissions. Adam: full edit/publish rights on testimonials and FAQ content. Maxxim/agency: full edit/publish rights on structural and technical content, with a review-before-publish rule for anything HIGH-CONTROL-adjacent as above.

Content templates needed. A testimonial-entry template (name, suburb, quote, situation tag — downsizer/estate-health-crisis/licence-loss-adjacent, for the situation-organised display in page-strategy.html); an FAQ-entry template (question, plain-language answer, optional schema tag); a What's Your Car Worth? recap-entry template (date, topic, short summary).

9. Testing & QA

Browser testing matrix. Current two major versions of Chrome, Safari, Firefox and Edge, on both desktop and mobile — with particular attention to Safari on iOS and Chrome on Android, the two most likely mobile browsers for this audience and their adult-child influencers.

Device testing. A representative older-model smartphone (not only the newest flagship device) at default, unadjusted text size, plus at least one test at an enlarged system text size — this audience is more likely than average to have increased their device's default text size, and the layout must not break when they have.

Load testing. Not a significant risk given expected traffic volumes for a single-operator local service business; a basic check that the static site serves quickly under a simulated moderate concurrent-visitor spike (e.g. following a radio mention) is sufficient — no dedicated load-testing tool is required.

Accessibility testing. Automated: an axe-core or equivalent automated WCAG scan on every page template before launch. Manual: the older-test-user-cohort task test named in section 5 ("find the number and call," "submit a callback request"), plus a manual screen-reader pass (VoiceOver or NVDA) on the callback form and navigation specifically.

User acceptance testing. Adam tests every page against this brief directly before launch, focused on: does every phone number instance work as a tap-to-call link; does the callback form submit correctly to the lead log and trigger his email notification; is any HIGH-CONTROL-adjacent copy (valuation, settlement) worded as he'd want it stated. Sign-off: Adam's written or verbal confirmation that all pages are approved for launch.

Staging environment. A Cloudflare Pages preview URL, generated automatically by the web-deploy skill's preview-then-promote flow, mirroring production exactly (same build, same content) until promoted — Adam reviews on this preview URL before the promote step.

10. Launch plan

Pre-launch checklist. All 15 pages' content loaded and reviewed; the callback form tested end-to-end (submission reaches the lead log and triggers a notification); web analytics and call tracking verified live and firing correctly; LocalBusiness JSON-LD validated (for example via Google's Rich Results Test); sitemap.xml, robots.txt and llms.txt all in place; the full 301 redirect map (section 7) tested against every current-site URL; SSL certificate active; accessibility automated scan passed with no critical issues; Adam's UAT sign-off received.

DNS changeover. Point adamhallbuymycar.com.au's DNS to the new Cloudflare Pages deployment via Maxxim's domain-setup skill. Lower the domain's TTL 24–48 hours before the planned cutover to speed propagation; monitor propagation status across major DNS resolvers before considering the changeover complete; keep the old hosting environment live and unmodified for at least 2 weeks post-cutover as a fallback.

Redirect mapping activation and verification. Activate the full 301 map at the moment of DNS cutover, not before or after; verify every mapped URL manually (or via an automated crawl) within 24 hours of cutover, checking for 200-status resolution to the correct new-site page and no redirect chains or loops.

Monitoring. Uptime monitoring via Cloudflare's built-in status alerts; error tracking via the analytics platform's error/404 reporting; Core Web Vitals monitored via Search Console's Core Web Vitals report, checked at least monthly.

Post-launch support. 30 days of included minor-fix support (content typos, broken-link fixes, minor styling issues) from Maxxim/agency following launch; response time within 2 business days for a non-urgent fix, same-day for anything affecting the phone number or callback form's function. Ongoing content maintenance beyond the 30 days follows the workflow in section 8.

Handover. Documentation: this brief plus a short "how to add a testimonial/FAQ entry" reference sheet, produced at the CMS training session in section 8. Credentials: domain registrar access, Cloudflare Pages access, analytics and Search Console access, all transferred or shared with Adam per his preference. Ongoing maintenance: covered by the same monthly reporting cadence already defined in measurement-plan.html, so website performance becomes one line in the business's existing monthly report rather than a separate, siloed check-in.
