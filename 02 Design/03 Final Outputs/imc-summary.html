IMC Summary

Integrated Strategy Overview

  • Every channel in the first quarter serves capacity, not demand. Campaign 1, "Prove the Engine," is instrumentation-only — no channel seeks a single incremental lead in months 1–3, because the business converts roughly 25 cars a month on trust alone with zero paid history, and the constraint is that the whole engine lives in one person's head.
  • 4CRB radio is the free, decade-proven anchor; referral and partnership are the cheapest next lever. Both run from day one, independent of any open client question.
  • Facebook and Google Search are the only genuinely gated channels — they wait for a three-part trigger (a month of usable lead-log data, plus CQ11/CQ06/CQ08 answered, plus the second-buyer conversation underway), not for a calendar date.
  • No public claim of "one number, never moved" appears anywhere in copy, PR or paid media until CQ01–CQ04 confirm the valuation rule. Every channel is written to hold without that claim.
  • The rebuilt, phone-first website is the precondition for everything measurable: its call tracking, analytics and lead log are what unlock SEO milestone tracking, paid-media activation, EDM automation and referral tracking.

Channel Integration Map

  • Awareness — 4CRB radio (decade of on-air credibility) plus earned PR and organic Facebook.
  • Consideration — rebuilt website (proof, testimonials, FAQ), SEO (LocalBusiness schema, regional pages), and referral/partnership word-of-mouth.
  • Conversion — the phone call is the single most important functional element across the whole system; the callback form is secondary; the home visit closes it.
  • Retention / advocacy — a settlement follow-up email carries the referral ask, feeding new advocates back into the referral channel.

Three critical handoffs:

  1. Radio credibility must be provable on the website the moment someone searches the name — "Adam's Story" and testimonials carry that handoff.
  2. The referral ask (post-settlement email, pending CQ07/CQ16) turns organic advocacy already happening (Rex, Kyogle; Terry, Pottsville; Jen, Currumbin Waters) into a systemised channel.
  3. The CRM lead log is the precondition every other channel's measurement depends on — SEO, paid media, PR and events all wait on the same instrumentation shipping first.

Website Summary

Full rebuild, not a redesign — the current site is a 5-page WordPress brochure with no analytics or schema. New build: static site (Astro-based), 15 pages, phone-first (persistent tel: link) with a 5-field callback form second, no valuation calculator ("a valuation is a home-visit judgement, not an algorithm"). WCAG 2.1 AA plus large type and large tap targets for a 60–75+ audience. Cloudflare Pages hosting, domain carried forward, Core Web Vitals green, full 301 redirect map for the current 5 pages, 30-day post-launch support. No fixed budget or date beyond "build now" — it is the precondition for CRM, SEO and paid media. See website-strategy.html, sitemap.html, website-brief.html.

Paid Media Summary

Facebook and Google Search only, and explicitly not yet activated — gated on the Now→Next trigger described above. Conservative: $0. Moderate: Facebook ~40%, Google Ads ~25% of an unset test budget. Two audiences: downsizers 55+ (Facebook only) and estate/health-crisis adult-children 35–55 (Facebook and Google Search). The phone call, not the form, is the conversion event; manual bidding for the first month; no CPA or ROAS benchmark exists yet (baseline TBD). Explicitly excluded: TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, X, Snapchat, Display, Performance Max, Shopping, YouTube ads, programmatic. See paid-media-plan.html.

SEO Summary

Baseline is zero — no rankings, backlinks or analytics exist today. 3-month milestone: site live and indexed, sitemap submitted, LocalBusiness schema verified, Search Console and analytics live, ranking baseline established. 6-month milestone: page-one on low-competition Northern Rivers and estate/health-crisis clusters, organic-attributed calls appearing in the monthly report. 12-month milestone: competitive visibility on harder Gold Coast/Brisbane head terms against CarBuyers and carsales, a public review presence established. Priority order: schema and technical foundations first, then the redirect map, speed and mobile. See seo-strategy.html.

Email & CRM Summary

CRM starts from zero — lead intake is phone-only today. A four-entity data model (contact, vehicle, enquiry, interaction) runs on a single flat spreadsheet at launch; the platform decision (spreadsheet vs. low-cost CRM tool) is deliberately left open pending budget. Two workflows only: lead assignment (100% to Adam today) and settlement follow-up/referral trigger. The CRM lead log is the precondition every other channel's reporting depends on.

EDM starts from zero list — one automation only: a settlement follow-up and referral-ask email, sent once, 3–5 days post-settlement. Two segments: settled customers and non-converted enquiries. No newsletter and no A/B testing in year one; single opt-in, large-type, mobile-first design for the 60–75+ audience. See crm-plan.html, edm-plan.html.

PR & Events Summary

Five PR story angles: the 27-year/radio milestone; the trust story ("no stranger in the room"); category heat (Cars4Us's reported acquisition, used only as market context); "what to do with a parent's car" service journalism; "we come to the Northern Rivers." Targets run three tiers, from the Gold Coast Bulletin and Courier-Mail down to community newsletters. Goal: zero PR during Campaign 1, then 2–4 placements per quarter from month 4 (hypothesis, no baseline). Outreach holds until permission to reference 10,000+ live valuations is secured.

Events: community-organisation speaking (Probus, CWA, Rotary, U3A, RSL), retirement-village touchpoints, small local sponsorship, a seniors' expo considered under Moderate only. Not recommended: owned events, trade shows, webinars. Q1 is groundwork only; first talk in Q2. See pr-plan.html, events-plan.html.

Traditional Media Summary

4CRB 89.3FM is confirmed as the anchor channel — a decade proven, already trusted, at effectively no incremental cost. No second station is recommended. TV, out-of-home and cinema are explicitly not recommended (cost, reach and audience mismatch). Print runs three narrow categories — seniors' publications, community newsletters and local press (Quest Newspapers, Northern Rivers regional papers) — via advertorial rather than display, inside the ~20% Moderate-scenario local/print line. Q1 is dark on print spend; the earliest illustrative single advertorial (Northern Rivers) sits in Q3, Moderate only. See traditional-media-plan.html.

Implementation Timeline

  • Month 1 (immediate, parallel): CRM lead log goes live — the precondition for every other workstream; design, website and photo/video production begin (design gated only on logo sign-off).
  • Months 1–3 — Campaign 1, "Prove the Engine": instrumentation only, near-zero spend, no new channel activated; website build in progress; SEO's technical foundation rides on the website build.
  • Month 3–4: website ships with analytics, call tracking and the callback form — this unlocks EDM automation, paid-media landing pages and SEO milestone tracking.
  • Months 4–9 — Campaign 2, "Handled With Care": referral ask goes live (pending CQ07/CQ16), estate/health-crisis content is piloted (pending CQ13), first PR and community-talk activity, Now→Next trigger evaluated for paid media.
  • Months 6–12 — Campaign 3, "We Come to the Rivers": Northern Rivers push, Q3 print advertorial (Moderate only), retirement-village partnerships.
  • Months 9–18+ — Campaign 4, "One Number, Never Moved" (gated, unscheduled): the guarantee claim launches only once CQ01–CQ04 are confirmed.

Critical path: paid media cannot start before the CRM lead log is live and CQ11/CQ06/CQ08 are answered — conditions, not a date. PR outreach holds until valuation-count usage permission is secured. Creative production is the one workstream that runs genuinely independent of open client questions, once the logo is signed off.
