Paid Media Plan

How to read this document

This is the execution blueprint for the two paid channels named in marketing-plan.html's Channel Strategy table — Facebook (paid) and Google Ads — and it builds directly on social-strategy.html section 8 (Paid Social Integration) and seo-strategy.html section 9 (AI Search & SGE Considerations) rather than re-deriving audience or platform rationale already established there.

The single most important thing this document says, stated up front: it should not be activated yet. marketing-plan.html and growth-roadmap.html are explicit that this is not a demand-generation engagement first — Adam Hall Buy My Car already converts roughly 25 cars a month on trust alone, with zero paid marketing history, and the constraint is capacity and measurement, not demand. Campaign 1 ("Prove the Engine", months 1–3, campaign-calendar.html) is instrumentation only; no paid spend runs in that window under either budget scenario. This plan exists so paid media can be picked up and switched on the moment the Now → Next advancement trigger in growth-roadmap.html is met — at least one full month of usable lead-log data, CQ11/CQ06/CQ08 answered, and a second-buyer conversation under way — not so spend begins on sign-off.

Budget is unconfirmed. CQ18 (marketing spend history) remains an open question pending Adam (recommended-next-moves.html). Every figure below is one of two scenarios: Conservative (near-zero — no paid spend, organic and measurement only) and Moderate (a modest, illustrative test budget, expressed only as a percentage split, never a fixed dollar figure). Paid channels appear only in the Moderate scenario, and only from the Next stage onward — this is a hard constraint carried from marketing-plan.html, not a suggestion.

1. Paid media objectives

Business goals this supports. Not volume maximisation. Paid media here supports Objective 4 in marketing-plan.html — growing qualified demand within the existing ~25-car-a-month capacity ceiling, deliberately capped rather than maximised, and Objective 4's wider goal of durable, trackable discovery. The illustrative target is new-channel leads (Facebook, Google, community, partnership combined) reaching a modest, named share (10–15%, hypothesis) of monthly volume by month 9–12 — paid media is one contributor to that share, not the whole of it.

Primary KPIs. Cost per click-through (Facebook); cost per booked home visit (both platforms, the metric that actually matters given the primary conversion event is a phone call, not a form — see conversion-pathway.html and customer-profile.html communication preferences); tracked phone-call conversions via mandatory call tracking (section 9).

Target CPA/ROAS. No benchmark is stated here. There is no historical paid-media performance for this business on any platform (funnel-review.html) — any figure quoted before a real test month would be invented precision, not a benchmark. The first full month of Moderate-scenario spend sets the working baseline, consistent with the "baseline TBD" approach used throughout measurement-plan.html.

Total budget range. Conservative: $0, paid channels off entirely. Moderate: a small monthly test budget, split by illustrative percentage only (section 7) — the actual figure is set with Adam once a budget envelope is confirmed, not assumed here.

Campaign timeframe. Not before the Next stage (months 3–9, growth-roadmap.html), and only once all three Now → Next trigger conditions hold together. A date alone is not the trigger — the conditions are.

4. Campaign structure

Account structure. A single ad account per platform — no case for multiple accounts at this scale.

Naming convention. AdamHall_[Platform]_[Objective]_[Audience]_[Region]_[YYYYMM] — for example AdamHall_FB_Traffic_Downsizer_GC-BNE-NR_202609.

Campaign → ad set/ad group structure. Facebook: two campaigns at launch — Downsizer prospecting and Estate/Health-Crisis prospecting — each with a single ad set; no retargeting ad set until the website rebuild ships a Meta Pixel. Google: one Search campaign, three ad groups by geography/intent cluster (Gold Coast, Brisbane, Northern Rivers-and-estate), mapped directly to the keyword clusters in seo-strategy.html section 3.

Audience segmentation. Prospecting only at launch on both platforms. Retargeting is deliberately deferred until website analytics exist (Objective 4, marketing-plan.html) — there is nothing to retarget against today.

Geographic and scheduling settings. Geo-fenced strictly to Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers postcodes on both platforms — no exceptions. Day-parting should be matched to Adam's actual availability to take a call, not run 24/7 by default; generating enquiries he cannot answer promptly directly undermines the capacity ceiling already named in funnel-review.html and sales-process-review.html.

8. Bidding & optimisation strategy

Facebook. Start on a manual, capped daily budget with a Traffic or Leads objective optimising toward phone click-throughs or Messenger contact — not an Awareness or Engagement objective, which would optimise for the wrong outcome entirely for a phone-first business.

Google Ads. Start on manual CPC, not an automated Target CPA or Target ROAS strategy — there is no conversion history to feed an automated bid strategy responsibly. Move to Maximise Conversions only once enough call-tracking data exists (a minimum of several tracked conversions) to trust the signal driving the algorithm.

Optimisation target. The phone call — initiated or, ideally, resulting in a booked home visit — is the optimisation target throughout, on both platforms. This is a hard constraint: the primary conversion mechanism for this business is a phone call, not a form (conversion-pathway.html; customer-profile.html), and any bidding strategy optimising toward a form fill or a generic click misreads what actually matters here.

Automated vs. manual bidding rationale. Manual first, on both platforms, for the entire first test month, regardless of scenario — automating a bid strategy against zero historical data would hand the algorithm a blind guess rather than a signal.

Learning phase expectations. A minimum of one full calendar month before judging performance on either platform; a minimum of a handful of tracked conversions (calls or booked visits) before considering any automated bidding switch on Google.

9. Tracking & attribution

Conversion tracking setup. Meta Pixel and Google Ads conversion tracking, both installed as part of the website rebuild (website-strategy.html), not before. Call tracking is mandatory and non-negotiable, carried as a hard constraint from the top of this plan: since the primary conversion event for this business is a phone call, not a form (customer-profile.html; conversion-pathway.html), no paid spend should go live without a dedicated tracked phone number — or dynamic number insertion by campaign/source — attributing calls to the specific campaign, ad group or keyword that generated them. This mirrors the identical requirement already stated for organic search in seo-strategy.html section 10.

Conversion events, in priority order. Phone call initiated (primary — the one event this whole plan is built to attribute correctly); booked home visit (secondary, harder to automate and likely requiring manual cross-reference against the lead log per measurement-plan.html); a website callback-request form, if one is added, as a tertiary, lower-priority event.

UTM conventions. utm_source=facebook|google, utm_medium=paid-social|cpc, utm_campaign=[naming convention, section 4], utm_content=[creative variant].

Attribution model. Last-click is an acceptable, honest choice given the low absolute volume and simple journey — more sophisticated multi-touch models would be false precision on this little data. Platform-reported conversions must always be reconciled against the manually tagged lead log (measurement-plan.html) as the actual source of truth, since no platform pixel can see a phone call that never touches the website.

Cross-platform tracking. A GA4-equivalent analytics setup (once the website rebuild ships) plus the call-tracking dashboard, both reconciled monthly against the single lead log — one source of truth across every channel, per the Channel Integration section of marketing-plan.html, rather than each platform keeping its own separate record.

Privacy and consent. A cookie-consent mechanism is required once tracking pixels go live. Any customer data captured via call tracking, a callback form, or ad-platform lead forms is personal information under the Australian Privacy Act — the specific compliance mechanism (consent wording, data-retention period, disclosure statement) is flagged here for direct legal-compliance review, not invented in this document. See crm-plan.html section 8 for the fuller data-handling flag, which this plan defers to rather than duplicates.

Key metrics to report. Spend, click-throughs, cost per click-through, tracked calls, cost per booked visit, and — where traceable via the lead log — cost per settled sale.

Optimisation triggers. Pause an ad set or ad group with no tracked call after a full month of spend at the planned pace; shift budget toward whichever platform's cost-per-booked-visit is consistently better after one full test month.

Escalation thresholds. Escalate to Adam if cost per booked visit is trending toward a level that would only make sense against a materially higher average retail gross than the confirmed ~$4,200 baseline (business-plan.html) — a sanity check specific to this business's real unit economics, not a generic industry ROAS rule.

A/B testing schedule. One test at a time — message, then audience, then creative variant — never run simultaneously given the small absolute reach involved (section 5).
