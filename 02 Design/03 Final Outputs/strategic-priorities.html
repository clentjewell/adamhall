Strategic Priorities

How to read this document

These are the four bets this engagement is committing to for the Design-phase horizon — not a wish list, and not everything worth doing. Each is force-ranked, traced to named evidence in the approved Discover documents, and paired with an explicit not-doing list, because focus is only real when it displaces something. The headline version of this ranking sits in business-plan.html; the staged rollout of these bets into Now/Next/Later horizons is in growth-roadmap.html; the metrics that prove each bet is working are in measurement-plan.html.

Bet 1 — Document Adam's judgement into a transferable Operating System

The bet. Write down, test and confirm every piece of judgement currently locked in Adam's head — valuation logic, the yes/no instinct, trust technique, payment and settlement discipline — so the business can be run, hired-into and eventually sold.

Why this, why now. This is problem 1 of 5 in priority-problems.html, ranked highest because intelligence-brief-digest.html states plainly that the undocumented system "makes the business unhireable-into, capacity-capped and unsellable." It directly serves Objective 2 in objectives-key-results.html ("turn Adam's judgement into a documented, transferable operating system") and is the mechanism behind the thesis discover-summary.html and success-definition.html both open on: freedom and a sale are the same build. It is timely now because the CORE mapping sessions are already running at two a week (phase1-work-digest.html) and every other Design decision — brand naming, capacity planning, public claims — depends on what these sessions confirm.

What winning looks like. All 22 CORE questions (CQ01–CQ22) answered verbatim and confirmed in writing with Adam; the payment-to-settlement sequence documented and confirmed (HIGH-CONTROL, CQ04); a second buyer identified and onboarding begun against the Adam Hall Standard. Tied to the "capacity documented" success statement in success-definition.html: a second buyer handling a defined share of visits unsupervised within 12 months of Design-phase sign-off.

What it displaces. Any demand-generation or paid-media investment before capacity exists to serve it; any public claim about valuation logic or settlement ahead of Adam's direct confirmation; locking brand or website work before the naming decision (CQ11) this documentation process will surface. This bet explicitly is not a race to add volume — success-definition.html rules that out directly.

The first 90 days. Continue CORE mapping at the existing twice-weekly cadence, with CQ11 (naming), CQ06 (lead source) and CQ08 (sale-readiness definition) prioritised per recommended-next-moves.html; open the conversation on second-buyer identification; begin — but do not publish — the payment-to-settlement documentation with Jewell Projects' engagement lead, under HIGH-CONTROL discipline throughout.

Bet 2 — Instrument the business before optimising it

The bet. Stand up basic measurement — lead volume, source, conversion rates, Adam-hours per deal — so every later marketing and growth decision is evidence-based, not guessed.

Why this, why now. Problem 2 in priority-problems.html: funnel-review.html concludes explicitly that "measurement itself is the weakest stage." It is Objective 1 in objectives-key-results.html — deliberately first, because no other objective's key results can be judged against a baseline until this one is live. It is timely now because it requires no CORE answers and no new tooling to start, making it the lowest-cost, highest-value first step named in recommended-next-moves.html.

What winning looks like. Lead volume, lead source split, lead-to-visit and visit-to-sale conversion rates tracked monthly from month 1; Adam-hours per deal and days lead-to-settlement logged on every transaction from month 1. Full definitions, baselines and ownership in measurement-plan.html.

What it displaces. Any decision to increase marketing spend or channel investment based on assumption rather than tracked data; any claim about which channel "works" (radio vs. word of mouth vs. website) before the source split is actually confirmed.

The first 90 days. A simple lead log or call register live immediately, no new tooling required; every incoming enquiry tagged with its source from day one, even informally; the first monthly report assembled once a full month of data exists.

Bet 3 — Systemise the referral ask

The bet. Turn the organic customer loyalty already evident in testimonials into a tested, repeatable, tracked referral ask in Adam's own words.

Why this, why now. Problem 3 in priority-problems.html: testimonials volunteer unprompted loyalty ("I will only sell my cars to Adam" — Rex, Kyogle) against a referral mechanism that is completely unconfirmed and untracked (CQ07, CQ16). It serves the third key result of Objective 1 in objectives-key-results.html and the "referral systemised" success statement in success-definition.html. funnel-review.html names this explicitly as "the cheapest available lever for volume growth without new spend," which makes it timely to start scaffolding as soon as CQ16 is answered, in parallel with Bets 1 and 2 rather than after them.

What winning looks like. A drafted and tested referral-ask script, live and tracked, generating a measurable, named share of monthly leads within 90 days of Design-phase sign-off (per success-definition.html); referral rate and referral source reported monthly from month 3 (Objective 1, key result 3).

What it displaces. Any paid-channel experimentation being treated as "the next lever to try" — this bet argues the cheapest, most proven lever is still sitting unsystemised, and should be exhausted before new spend is considered.

The first 90 days. Draft the referral-ask script the moment CQ16 is answered; test it on the next few completed sales without publishing it as a formal promise until proven; add a referral-source field to the lead log from Bet 2 from day one, even before the script exists, so the baseline is captured honestly.

Bet 4 — Claim the uncontested market position

The bet. Formalise named-person continuity and the radio-and-referral proof engine as public, quotable assets, and pilot messaging for the two segments and one geography no researched competitor visibly claims: estate/health-crisis sellers and Northern Rivers NSW.

Why this, why now. Problem 4 in priority-problems.html: competitor-analysis.html documents that every named direct competitor (CarBuyers, We Buy Cars, carsales Instant Offer) is a company or platform with rotating staff or a dealer panel, and differentiation-opportunities.html ranks named-person continuity and the proof engine as the two "lead moves" available. market-gaps.html confirms the estate/health-crisis segment and Northern Rivers coverage are open, structurally hard for the researched competitors to chase. This serves Objective 4 in objectives-key-results.html and the competitive-angle findings in discover-summary.html: this audience increasingly asks an AI assistant rather than typing a search term, and the position Adam already holds is currently unclaimed in market.

What winning looks like. The 10,000+ on-air valuations claim and existing testimonials formalised as a public proof asset (Objective 4, key result 1); an estate & health-crisis service track named and piloted in messaging (Objective 4, key result 3); named-person continuity as the leading "only we" line in brand and website work. The stronger, HIGH-CONTROL-adjacent claim — "one number, never moved" — sits as a strong second-tier move gated behind Bet 1's documentation, not claimed until provably true.

What it displaces. Competing on headline price against CarBuyers or carsales Instant Offer — explicitly ruled out in differentiation-opportunities.html and success-definition.html; any generic brand-awareness activity with no traceable link to a booked home visit; publishing the "one number, never moved" guarantee or a Northern Rivers volume claim before the evidence behind them exists.

The first 90 days. Secure Adam's written permission to use the 10,000+ valuations figure and existing testimonials as public marketing assets (a blocking input named in recommended-next-moves.html); draft — sensitively, ideally in Adam's own words once CQ13 is answered — early estate/health-crisis messaging for later piloting, without publishing prematurely.

Dependency picture

Bets 1 and 2 can run fully in parallel: Bet 2's measurement instrumentation depends on no CORE answer and can start immediately, while Bet 1's CORE mapping continues on its own schedule. Bet 3 depends on one specific CORE answer (CQ16) surfaced inside Bet 1, but its scaffolding — the referral-source field in the lead log — should start under Bet 2 from day one, so the baseline exists the moment the script goes live. Bet 4 partially depends on Bet 1: the proof-engine and estate/health-crisis moves can proceed independently once written permission is secured, but the stronger "one number, never moved" claim is fully gated behind Bet 1's valuation-rule documentation being consistent enough to hold. Bet 4 also connects to, but does not resolve, the brand-naming decision (CQ11) — a Discover→Design gate dependency that sits outside these four bets and must close before brand-strategy.html or logo-brief.html begin.

In growth-roadmap.html terms: Bets 1 and 2 anchor the Now stage; Bet 3 spans Now into Next; Bet 4 is primarily a Next-stage deliverable, with its strongest claim reserved for Later.
