Positioning

How to read this document

This is the position Adam Hall Buy My Car claims and defends in market. It commits to, rather than re-argues, the category read already established in category-positioning.html: the crowded corner is price-led and company- or platform-delivered; the open corner is trust-and-ease-maximising and fully human. The brand platform this position sits inside lives in brand-strategy.html; the message house and offer ladder built from it live in messaging-offer-architecture.html.

One note carried over from brand-strategy.html: whether the brand permanently carries Adam's personal name (CQ11) is unresolved and is not decided here. The position below is written so it holds under the current name and does not depend on that decision — it is a position about a way of doing business (named-person, home-visit, honest-number), not a claim that only works if the master brand is a specific name forever.

The positioning statement

For car sellers aged 60–75 and older across the Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers who want relief rather than a negotiation, Adam Hall Buy My Car is the trust-and-ease-maximising, fully personal way to sell a car — the one where the same known person handles the whole thing, at your home, with a number meant to hold — because Adam personally attends every visit, backed by 27+ years in the trade and a decade of public, on-air valuation credibility, in a category where every named competitor is a company, a branch network or a dealer panel, not a person.

Broken into its parts:

  • For [audience] — sellers aged 60–75 and older across the Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers, plus the adult children who influence or decide on their behalf for estate and health-driven sales (customer-segments.html, audience-teardown.html). [Segment ages and boundaries are hypothesis, not yet volume-tested with Adam.]
  • [Brand] is the [frame] — the trust-and-ease-maximising, fully human and personal way to sell a car, as opposed to a price-maximising or self-service/digital-first way (category-positioning.html positioning map).
  • that [differentiator] — one known, accountable individual handles the entire transaction personally, at the seller's home, rather than a rotating inspector, call centre or dealer panel (differentiation-opportunities.html, "named-person continuity").
  • because [proof] — 27+ years of automotive trade experience, and 10,000+ live, on-air valuations delivered since 2010 as host of "What's Your Car Worth?" on 4CRB 89.3FM (confirmed fact, category-positioning.html, offer-worksheet.html) — a public reputation asset no researched competitor can claim at any price.

The "could a competitor say this?" test. No researched competitor can say this and mean it. CarBuyers and We Buy Cars are companies delivered through branches, panels or rotating staff; carsales Instant Offer routes the seller to a nationwide accredited dealer network, not to carsales itself (competitor-analysis.html). Copying the claim in words would require any of them to abandon the very branch, panel or platform structure that lets them operate at national or multi-branch scale — this is a structural barrier, not a marketing gap they could close with a campaign (category-positioning.html, "why incumbents won't easily follow").

What would falsify the position

The evidence or market shift that would break this claim, and the signals to watch so the brand repositions before customers notice.

  • A price that moves after the home visit, even once, made public. Because "one number, never moved" is the sharpest available differentiator against the category's most cited complaint (competitor-analysis.html), a single well-publicised instance of the number changing after inspection would be more damaging to this brand than to a competitor already expected to do it. Signal to watch: any customer complaint, review or word-of-mouth report describing a post-visit price change, however small the sample.
  • Volume growth that requires a rotating or unaccountable second buyer before the Adam Hall Standard is documented and trained to. The position depends on "the same known person" — introducing a second buyer who is not visibly trained and accountable under the same Standard would quietly convert this brand into exactly the panel-delivered model it currently beats (differentiation-opportunities.html). Signal to watch: customer feedback that distinguishes "Adam" from "whoever they sent", or any drop in referral rate coinciding with a new hire.
  • A competitor credibly adopting a named-person or "number doesn't move" model. The position's defensibility rests on this being structurally hard for CarBuyers, We Buy Cars and carsales Instant Offer to copy at their current scale (category-positioning.html) — but a new, smaller entrant built specifically around a single trusted local buyer could contest this ground directly. Signal to watch: any new Gold Coast/Brisbane/Northern Rivers competitor marketing on a named individual rather than a company name.
  • Public review volume remaining at zero while competitors' grows. A trust-first position that cannot be verified independently online becomes harder to defend the longer competitors accumulate visible, if imperfect, review volume (CarBuyers already has 184 reviews). Signal to watch: the gap between Adam Hall Buy My Car's independent review count and competitors' widening rather than closing.
  • The brand-naming decision (CQ11) resolving in a way that is not carried through consistently. If Adam later decides to generalise the brand beyond his personal name (brand-strategy.html, Scenario B or C), a half-finished transition — old name in some places, new name in others, no clear explanation to existing customers — would confuse the exact trust this position is built on. Signal to watch: any customer-facing inconsistency in how the brand refers to itself once a transition begins.
