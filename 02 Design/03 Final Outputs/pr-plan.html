PR Plan

How to read this document

This is the earned-media plan for Adam Hall Buy My Car — a home-visit direct car-buying service across the Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers built on 27+ years of trade experience and a decade of public credibility from the “What’s Your Car Worth?” segment on 4CRB 89.3FM (10,000+ live, on-air valuations since 2010). It is built on brand-strategy.html, positioning.html, customer-profile.html, content-strategy.html, competitor-analysis.html and differentiation-opportunities.html, and is sequenced against marketing-plan.html and campaign-calendar.html. It does not repeat the channel strategy, campaign framework or budget scenarios set out there — it sits inside them.

Three ground rules carried through every section below. First, this business has no PR history, no existing media relationships beyond the organic 4CRB partnership, and no confirmed budget for PR activity (marketing-plan.html notes marketing spend actuals are an open CORE question). Every outlet named below is a recommended target type, not an existing relationship or a confirmed commitment — no journalist is named, and no outlet has agreed to anything described here. Second, demand-generating PR is deliberately gated behind the capacity-and-measurement work in marketing-plan.html’s Campaign 1 (“Prove the Engine”): that campaign is asset-formalisation only, not a press push, because the business already converts on trust with no tracked lead engine and a one-person capacity ceiling of roughly 25 cars a month. Third, several proof points this plan wants to use publicly — the “one number, never moved” guarantee, and any high-control detail of the valuation or settlement process — are not yet confirmed as consistently true and must not be stated in any pitch or press material until Adam confirms them directly, per positioning.html and differentiation-opportunities.html.

1. PR Objectives

Media coverage goals. No PR activity has run before, so there is no baseline to set a confident placement target against. The realistic goal for the first year is modest and staged: zero press activity during Campaign 1 (asset formalisation only); once permission for the 10,000+ valuations claim and testimonials is secured, a working target of 2–4 local or seniors-outlet placements per quarter from month 4 onward, reviewed and reset once the first quarter of real outreach data exists. [Hypothesis — no PR track record to benchmark against.]

Share of voice. No researched competitor (CarBuyers, We Buy Cars, carsales Instant Offer) is known to run local or human-interest PR in this market — competitor-analysis.html found no evidence of it either way. Share-of-voice tracking is therefore a measurement habit to build from month 1 of activity, not a target with a baseline today.

Key publications the client should appear in. Prioritise outlets that reach a 60–75+ audience directly, rather than general business or motoring trade press: regional daily and community papers across the Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers, and dedicated seniors’-interest publications. The full list and rationale sit in the Target Media List below.

Thought leadership positioning. Adam should be known in media as the honest, plain-spoken local voice on selling a car later in life — someone who explains a number rather than imposing one, and who has spent 27 years in the trade and a decade proving it live on the radio. This positioning is the same one positioning.html and brand-strategy.html establish for every other channel; PR’s job is to make it visible in earned media, not to invent a separate media persona.

2. Key Story Angles

Angle 1 — The 27-year trade veteran and the radio milestone

Hook. A local tradesman who has quietly valued more than 10,000 cars live on air, for free, since 2010 — and still does the same honest number at the kitchen table.

Why newsworthy. Genuine human interest and a round, verifiable milestone (10,000+ valuations, confirmed per positioning.html); ties a long-running local media fixture to a real local business story rather than a straight advertisement.

Target outlet types. Regional daily papers’ local business or human-interest desks; seniors’-interest publications; talkback or local ABC radio human-interest segments.

Timing. Once written permission to use the 10,000+ valuations figure and testimonials is formally secured (Campaign 1 KPI, marketing-plan.html) — not before, since the claim must be provably confirmed before it is pitched as a public fact.

Angle 2 — The trust story: selling a car without a stranger in the room

Hook. A consented customer story showing what a home-visit, no-haggle sale actually looks like for an older seller who dreaded the alternative — built from an existing testimonial (for example the confirmed Currumbin Waters story referenced in content-strategy.html, Pillar 4) once fresh, specific consent is obtained for media use.

Why newsworthy. Genuine human interest with an emotional throughline (relief, dignity, being treated fairly at a hard moment) rather than a product story; matches the estate/health-crisis segment’s real weight per customer-profile.html.

Target outlet types. Seniors’-interest publications; local community papers’ human-interest sections.

Timing. Only once a named customer has given specific, informed consent for their story to appear in press (a different, higher bar than website/testimonial consent) — this is sensitive territory and must never be pitched opportunistically around a death, illness or care transition, per content-strategy.html’s Pillar 4 caution.

Angle 3 — Category heat: what a $120 million sale means for people selling a car later in life

Hook. Cars4Us — the marketplace business publicly reported to have grown to roughly $500 million in scale and been acquired by Toyota for roughly $120 million, and named in this business’s own discovery material as its long-term growth comparable (differentiation-opportunities.html) — is a sign the used-car buying category is attracting serious capital. Use that news heat as a hook for a feature on what a fast-consolidating, increasingly platform-driven category means for an older seller who just wants one honest person, not an app.

Why newsworthy. Timeliness (real, publicly reported industry news) and a genuine point of contrast — a personal, single-operator model against a marketplace-and-platform trend. This angle references Cars4Us and Toyota only as public category context; it does not imply, and must not be pitched as implying, any relationship, partnership or endorsement between Adam Hall Buy My Car and either company.

Target outlet types. Regional business pages; consumer-affairs or money-and-me sections that cover cost-of-living and asset-selling decisions for older readers.

Timing. Opportunistic — pitched if and when further category-consolidation news breaks, rather than tied to a fixed date.

Angle 4 — Service journalism: what to do with a parent’s car

Hook. A practical, non-salesy explainer for adult children managing a parent’s downsizing, health decline or estate — “what to actually do with the car” — written as genuine service journalism, not a disguised pitch, and drawing on the same ground already planned for content-strategy.html Pillar 4.

Why newsworthy. Practical utility for a reader in a stressful moment, and a real market gap — no researched competitor visibly addresses this segment (differentiation-opportunities.html, move 3). Well suited to a contributed or byline piece rather than a straight news pitch.

Target outlet types. Seniors’-interest publications with a family-carer readership; regional papers’ lifestyle or seniors sections.

Timing. Once Adam’s own words on how he currently handles these situations are captured (CQ13, per content-strategy.html) — the piece should read as lived experience, not a marketing brief.

Angle 5 — “We come to the Northern Rivers”

Hook. A local business explicitly extending a personal, home-visit service into a region (Northern Rivers NSW) that no researched competitor visibly serves (market-gaps.html, referenced in differentiation-opportunities.html).

Why newsworthy. Local relevance and a genuine gap-filling story for Northern Rivers-specific outlets, who cover local business and service availability as a matter of course.

Target outlet types. Northern Rivers regional papers and community publications.

Timing. Once current Northern Rivers volume is confirmed with Adam, so the coverage claim is not overstated relative to actual capacity — matching the gating already set for Campaign 3 in marketing-plan.html and campaign-calendar.html.

3. Target Media List

No outlet below has an existing relationship or agreement with Adam Hall Buy My Car (with the sole exception of 4CRB, covered as an owned/organic radio relationship in traditional-media-plan.html and partnership-referral-plan.html, not as an earned-media target here). All are recommended target types to research and approach, not confirmed placements.

Tier 1 — Regional metro dailies and major digital outlets

Gold Coast Bulletin, the Courier-Mail (Brisbane) and Brisbane Times — specifically their local business, human-interest or consumer/lifestyle sections rather than motoring news. Approach: a considered, exclusive-first pitch for the strongest single story (most likely Angle 1 or Angle 2) rather than a mass press release, since a personal, human-interest story earns more from one well-placed exclusive than from a wide, generic send.

Tier 2 — Industry, trade and seniors’-interest publications

Seniors’-interest outlets read directly by this audience — YourLifeChoices and Starts at 60 are established Australian seniors’-focused publications with the right readership fit; National Seniors Australia and COTA (Council on the Ageing) member communications are worth researching for contributed-content opportunities given their advocacy focus on exactly this audience’s concerns. Automotive trade press exists but is a poor fit here — this is a consumer human-interest story, not a trade story, so it is deliberately not prioritised. Approach: contributed content and byline pitches (Angle 4 fits this tier best), plus reactive commentary offers tied to any car-selling-scam or cost-of-living news these outlets run.

Tier 3 — Local, community and Northern Rivers outlets

Quest Newspapers’ community titles across Brisbane and Gold Coast suburbs; Northern Rivers regional and community papers (for example titles covering Lismore, Byron and Tweed) for Angle 5 specifically; and community-organisation newsletters (RSL sub-branches, Rotary, Probus, CWA, retirement villages) reached via the same partner relationships being built in partnership-referral-plan.html, which double as a low-cost, high-trust media channel in their own right. Approach: press release plus a direct, warm introduction wherever a partnership contact already exists; these are lower-formality, higher-trust placements than Tier 1 or 2.

Confirm exact current mastheads, sections and submission requirements directly before any pitch — regional media ownership and section names change; nothing above should be treated as a verified, current media kit.

4. Thought Leadership Strategy

Spokesperson positioning. Adam is the sole spokesperson today, credible on the strength of 27+ years in the trade and a decade of public, on-air valuation history — consistent with the “stay in the room” and named-person continuity themes running through brand-strategy.html and differentiation-opportunities.html. No second spokesperson exists yet; if a second, values-trained buyer is introduced under the Adam Hall Standard in future, any media positioning for them must wait until they are genuinely trained and accountable under that standard, not introduced as a marketing convenience (per differentiation-opportunities.html’s explicit caution).

Key topics of expertise. Honest, plain-language car valuation; protecting older sellers from pressure tactics and lowball post-inspection price drops (the category’s most cited complaint, per competitor-analysis.html); what to do with a car during a downsizing move or an estate/health transition; the state of the used-car buying category as it consolidates around larger platforms.

Commentary opportunities. Reactive commentary when a used-car scam, a consumer-affairs warning about pressure-selling, or further category-consolidation news breaks; cost-of-living stories where selling an asset is part of a household’s decision. Turnaround expectations must respect that Adam is a solo operator running the business day-to-day — a realistic commentary turnaround target is 48–72 hours, not same-day, and any faster ask should be politely declined rather than rushed into an inconsistent public statement.

Byline and op-ed targets. Seniors’-interest publications (YourLifeChoices, Starts at 60, National Seniors Australia/COTA member channels) are the most realistic targets for a contributed piece, since they routinely run practical, first-person-adjacent content from local experts. No outlet has confirmed it accepts contributed content from this business — this must be established directly before drafting a byline pitch.

Speaking engagements. Community-organisation talks (Probus, CWA, Rotary, U3A, RSL sub-branches) are the realistic near-term speaking opportunity and are covered in full, including venue, format and follow-up, in events-plan.html — not repeated here. Any genuine industry-conference speaking opportunity is out of scope for this Design-phase plan; there is no evidence this audience or this business’s capacity supports it.

Should establish credibility (27 years in the trade, decade on 4CRB) and warmth (plain-spoken, local, not corporate) in the same voice described in brand-strategy.html’s tone-of-voice guide — drafted from Adam’s own words wherever possible rather than generic corporate biography language.

No revenue, volume or growth figures from internal discovery material should appear in any public-facing fact sheet without Adam’s explicit sign-off, since these are commercially sensitive, not public proof points.

Image and asset requirements. High-resolution, real photography as above; at least one clean headshot of Adam suitable for print reproduction; a small library of consented customer or driveway/home-visit imagery once available, coordinated with any photo/video brief commissioned separately.

6. Media Outreach Plan

Approach per tier. Tier 1 — a personalised, exclusive-first pitch to a single outlet for the strongest available story, never a mass send. Tier 2 — a considered pitch or contributed-content proposal, tailored to each seniors’-interest outlet’s actual content style. Tier 3 — a press release plus a warm introduction wherever a partnership contact exists (per partnership-referral-plan.html), since local and community outlets respond well to a known local connection.

Pitch structure guidelines. Subject line stating the human story plainly, not a marketing headline (“A Gold Coast tradesman has valued 10,000 cars live on the radio — and still gives the same honest number at your door”, not “Adam Hall Buy My Car launches new campaign”); the hook in the first line; supporting detail (confirmed facts only, per Angle sections above) in the second paragraph; a clear, low-pressure call to action (an offer of an interview, a photo opportunity, or supporting material) rather than a hard ask.

Follow-up cadence. One follow-up after 5–7 business days if there is no response; a second, final follow-up after a further 7–10 days if the story is genuinely time-sensitive; otherwise let it go rather than over-pursuing a single outlet, consistent with the unhurried, non-pressuring brand voice this business is built on.

Exclusivity strategy. Angle 1 (the 27-year/10,000-valuations milestone) and Angle 2 (the consented trust story) are the two strongest candidates for a genuine single-outlet exclusive, offered to whichever Tier 1 outlet shows the strongest fit once approached. Angles 3, 4 and 5 are better suited to a considered, non-exclusive pitch to the relevant Tier 2/3 outlets, since their value is in reach across seniors’-interest and local audiences rather than a single high-profile placement.

7. Digital PR & Content

SEO-driven PR content. Once the website rebuild is underway (website-strategy.html, once commissioned), the strongest linkable asset this business can offer is the formalised 10,000+ valuations proof page itself — a single, citable, well-sourced page that any outlet covering Angle 1 can link back to, feeding both PR credibility and seo-strategy.html’s backlink goals. No data study, tool or ranked list is proposed here, since none of that content exists or is planned — the proof asset is the honest linkable asset this business actually has.

Newsjacking approach. Watch for used-car scam warnings, consumer-affairs coverage of pressure-selling tactics, and further used-car category consolidation news (in the spirit of Angle 3); respond only where Adam’s genuine expertise adds something real, never simply to attach the brand name to a trending story.

Reactive commentary process. Adam approves every word attributed to him before it goes to a journalist, given the trust-first position this brand depends on; realistic turnaround is 48–72 hours as noted above, reflecting his solo capacity — this should be stated plainly to any journalist asking for faster comment rather than promising a turnaround the business cannot honour.

Social amplification. Any earned coverage should be shared on the business’s own Facebook Page per social-strategy.html, framed as gratitude and proof (“lovely to see this local story picked up”) rather than self-congratulation, consistent with the brand’s warm, unhurried tone.

8. Crisis Communication Framework

Potential reputation risks, specific to this business:

  • A customer or family publicly reports that a home-visit price changed after inspection. Per positioning.html, this is named as the single scenario most damaging to this brand specifically, because “one number, never moved” is the sharpest claimed differentiator — a public instance, however small the sample, would be disproportionately damaging.
  • A complaint from the estate/health-crisis segment describing the process as pressuring or insensitive during a vulnerable moment — the highest-care, highest-reputational-risk segment this business serves (customer-profile.html, content-strategy.html Pillar 4).
  • A prominent first negative online review. The business currently has zero independent public review presence (positioning.html) — a single visible negative review, in the absence of any offsetting review volume, would carry disproportionate weight until a genuine review base is built.
  • Confusion with, or impersonation by, an unrelated car-buying operator trading on a similar “we buy your car” or radio-adjacent claim, given how generic this category’s naming conventions are (competitor-analysis.html).
  • A data-handling or paperwork issue touching identity verification, PPSR or rego transfer — all named as HIGH-CONTROL items in the underlying discovery material and therefore carrying real regulatory and reputational weight if mishandled.

Holding statements (framework, not scripted text). For any pricing complaint: acknowledge the concern directly, commit to reviewing the specific case personally with the customer, and avoid any defensive language minimising their experience. For any estate/health-crisis complaint: lead with empathy and ownership, offer a direct personal conversation rather than a written statement first. For a negative review: respond personally, briefly, and without defensiveness, per the Adam Hall Standard’s “ownership, not excuses” value (brand-strategy.html).

Spokesperson protocols. Adam is the sole spokesperson and sole approver of any public statement today — there is no delegate, which is itself a capacity risk worth naming: if Adam is unreachable during a live issue, there is currently no one else authorised to respond. This should be resolved once a second, values-trained buyer exists under the Adam Hall Standard (brand-strategy.html), not assumed sooner.

Social media monitoring during a crisis. No social-listening tool exists or is budgeted; the realistic near-term process is manual — checking the business’s own Facebook Page and a simple web search/Google Alert for the business name on a daily basis during any live issue, escalating to a fuller response process only if something is found.

Post-crisis review process. After any issue is resolved, a short internal review with Adam (what happened, what was said, what changed) feeding directly into measurement-plan.html’s reporting cadence, so a pattern of complaints — not just a single incident — can be caught early.

10. Measurement

Media coverage tracking. No PR monitoring tool is budgeted or assumed; the realistic starting process is a simple manual log (outlet, date, story, link) maintained alongside the lead log described in measurement-plan.html, plus a basic web search or Google Alert for the business name.

Sentiment analysis. A simple manual positive/neutral/negative tag against each piece of coverage, reviewed monthly — not an automated sentiment tool, which is not justified at this budget and volume.

Share of voice. Cannot be tracked meaningfully today because no competitor PR baseline exists (competitor-analysis.html); this becomes a genuine metric only once several months of the business’s own coverage log exist to compare a competitor's future activity against.

Domain authority impact. Any backlink earned from online coverage should be logged and fed to seo-strategy.html once that plan is commissioned — every media placement is treated as a potential SEO asset, not just a standalone story.

Equivalent advertising value (EAV). Can be included as a supplementary metric once coverage exists, but should be reported alongside a clear caveat that it is a rough, industry-standard proxy, not a reliable measure of actual business value — it should never be the primary metric used to judge whether PR activity is working.

Reporting cadence. Monthly coverage summary folded into the same reporting cycle as marketing-plan.html and measurement-plan.html, avoiding a second, parallel reporting system; a quarterly strategic review against the objectives in Section 1 above.
