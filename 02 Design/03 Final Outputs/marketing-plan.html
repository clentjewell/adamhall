In-Market Activation Plan

How to read this document

This is the master go-to-market plan every channel plan hangs off. It translates business-plan.html, strategic-priorities.html and growth-roadmap.html into a coordinated plan of attack, and it is the document partnership-referral-plan.html, campaign-calendar.html, social-strategy.html and content-strategy.html are briefed from.

The single most important call this plan makes, stated up front: this is not a demand-generation plan first. strategic-priorities.html and growth-roadmap.html are explicit that Adam Hall Buy My Car's constraint is not demand — the business already converts roughly 25 cars a month on trust alone, with zero paid marketing history (USER.html; business-plan.html) — but capacity (one person runs every stage) and measurement (nothing is tracked). Every recommendation below is sequenced deliberately behind the capacity and measurement work in Bets 1 and 2, and growing demand ahead of that work is explicitly named as a risk to avoid, not a goal to chase (business-plan.html, Risk assessment).

Budget is unconfirmed. CQ18 (marketing spend history and actuals) and the Design-phase brand/website budget envelope are both open questions pending Adam (recommended-next-moves.html). This plan is therefore scenario-scaled — a Conservative scenario (near-zero net-new spend, time and reallocation only) and a Moderate scenario (a modest, illustrative monthly test budget) — rather than built against a fixed number. No figure in this plan should be read as a confirmed budget; all are ranges for planning discussion, flagged where they are.

Channels explicitly not recommended this phase

  • Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, X/Twitter. Confirmed audience mismatch for the primary segment; see social-strategy.html, section 2, for the full rationale.
  • Broad-reach paid display or programmatic. No evidence this audience responds to anonymous digital advertising, and it directly contradicts the named-person, trust-first position (positioning.html).
  • National press, TV or outdoor. Cost and reach mismatch for a three-region, capacity-capped business; radio already occupies this role at a fraction of the cost, with a decade of proof behind it.
  • Any channel investment ahead of the measurement baseline. Named explicitly as a risk in business-plan.html — no channel decision should be made "because it's popular" rather than because the lead-log data supports it.

Campaign framework

Four campaigns across the engagement horizon, deliberately sequenced so that Campaign 1 does no demand-generation at all — it exists to prove and formalise what already works, per the capacity-before-demand sequencing rule stated above. The year-view layout of these campaigns lives in campaign-calendar.html.

Campaign 1 — "Prove the Engine"

Objective. Instrument the business and formalise its existing proof assets — no new demand sought. Target segment(s). None directly targeted; this campaign is internal-facing and asset-building. Channels. Lead log/measurement (internal), website (proof asset placement), Facebook (soft launch only). Key message. N/A — this is foundation work, not a customer-facing message. KPIs. Lead log live and populated for a full month; written permission secured for the 10,000+ valuations claim and testimonials; CQ11, CQ06 and CQ08 answered (growth-roadmap.html, Now-stage "done" criteria). Budget range. Conservative: near-zero, time only. Moderate: unchanged — this campaign should not require net-new paid spend under either scenario. Timing. Months 1–3.

Campaign 2 — "Handled With Care" (estate/health-crisis pilot)

Objective. Pilot named messaging and a dedicated pathway for the estate/health-driven segment, converting an open market gap into a claimed one (Bet 4). Target segment(s). Estate/health-driven family sellers (Jen); reached via the adult-child decision-maker. Channels. Website (new dedicated section/page), Facebook (light-touch, careful cadence per social-strategy.html), partnership channels (see partnership-referral-plan.html), Google (Moderate scenario only, once the website exists). Key message. "We'll take care of it, so you don't have to." KPIs. Estate/health-crisis segment share of monthly volume, tracked once segment tagging exists in the lead log (measurement-plan.html); qualitative feedback on tone from the first handful of served families. Budget range. Conservative: content and partnership time only. Moderate: a small, targeted Facebook test budget (illustrative, see Budget allocation below). Timing. Months 4–9, gated behind CQ13 (Adam's own words) and the second-buyer conversation being under way.

Campaign 3 — "We Come to the Rivers" (Northern Rivers geography)

Objective. Claim explicit Northern Rivers NSW coverage — an open gap no researched competitor visibly serves (market-gaps.html). Target segment(s). Downsizers and estate/health-driven sellers specifically located in Northern Rivers. Channels. Local community/print-adjacent presence, partnership outreach to Northern Rivers-specific organisations (partnership-referral-plan.html), Facebook local groups, website area content (content-strategy.html, Pillar 5). Key message. "We come to you, wherever you are on the Gold Coast, Brisbane or the Northern Rivers." KPIs. Northern Rivers segment share of monthly volume (measurement-plan.html); number of Northern Rivers partner relationships established. Budget range. Conservative: partnership and content time only. Moderate: a modest local-community/print-adjacent spend (illustrative, see below). Timing. Months 6–12, sequenced after current Northern Rivers volume is confirmed with Adam so the claim is not overstated relative to actual capacity (differentiation-opportunities.html).

Campaign 4 — "One Number, Never Moved" (guarantee launch, gated)

Objective. Publicly launch the flagship differentiator once it is provably true, converting the category's sharpest weakness into Adam Hall Buy My Car's clearest strength (positioning.html). Target segment(s). All segments. Channels. Radio sign-off line, website hero claim, Facebook, any Moderate-scenario paid activity live at the time. Key message. "One visit. One number. No games." KPIs. Guarantee live and honoured with zero exceptions (binary, per measurement-plan.html); any shift in conversion rate or referral rate attributable to the launch. Budget range. Not yet set — this campaign is explicitly gated and should not be budgeted until its launch condition is met. Timing. Not before the valuation-rule documentation in Bet 1 is consistent enough to sustain the promise without exception (growth-roadmap.html, Later stage) — this must not be shortcut for a marketing deadline.

Brand vs. performance split. Under both scenarios, spend and effort should lean brand/trust-building (proof assets, content, radio, community presence) over direct-response performance media — consistent with a trust-and-ease-maximising position that would be undermined by price-led, urgency-driven performance tactics (positioning.html). An illustrative 80/20 brand-to-performance split is a reasonable starting point once any Moderate-scenario paid spend begins.

Contingency. Hold 10% of any confirmed Moderate-scenario budget in reserve for reallocation once the first quarter of real data shows which channel is actually converting, rather than locking a full year's split on hypothesis.

ROI expectations. Expressed as ranges, not false precision, because no channel here has a performance track record. At ~$4,200 average retail gross per car (business-plan.html), even a small number of incremental cars per month from a new channel could justify a modest test budget — but this should be judged against real lead-log data from month 2 onward, not assumed in advance.

Channel integration

Synergy example 1 — the radio-to-referral loop. A radio segment builds recognition (Exposure) → a testimonial from a well-served customer is formatted for Facebook and the website (Consideration) → the same customer is asked for a referral at settlement (Advocacy) → their referral becomes a new Exposure event for someone else, closing the loop without new spend. This is the single cheapest, most coherent channel synergy available to this business today.

Synergy example 2 — the estate/health-crisis handoff. A partnership contact (a solicitor, an aged-care transition service — see partnership-referral-plan.html) refers a family to the website's dedicated Pillar 4 content (Consideration) → the adult-child decision-maker finds the same message reinforced on Facebook if she searches or scrolls (Consideration) → she calls, and the same tone carries through to First Contact and the home visit (messaging-by-stage.html). Consistent messaging across all three touchpoints is what makes this handoff credible rather than jarring.

Synergy example 3 — the Northern Rivers geography claim. Local community/print-adjacent presence and partnership outreach establish credibility in a region with no direct competitor presence (market-gaps.html) → website area content and Facebook local-group activity reinforce the same claim digitally → any Moderate-scenario Google Ads test in that specific geography only begins once the claim is genuinely provable, avoiding an inconsistent or overstated coverage promise (differentiation-opportunities.html).

Data flow. Every channel above feeds one source of truth — the lead log defined in measurement-plan.html — rather than each channel keeping its own separate record. This is what makes the "one number that tells us the pathway is healthy" (lead-to-settled-sale conversion by source, per conversion-pathway.html) possible at all.
