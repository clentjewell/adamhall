CRM Brief

The job

Build a single, simple lead log from zero, distilled from crm-plan.html — replacing "nothing tracked" with one source of truth every channel feeds. There is no CRM or lead-capture system today; lead intake is phone-only. This is Objective 1's home document: no other marketing metric can be judged against a baseline until this log exists and is used from month 1.

Audience

Built for Adam operating alone today, with a second buyer expected to join within the Next-stage horizon. Every field and workflow must survive being used at 9pm after a long day of home visits — nothing here should require dedicated admin support or a training course.

The message

Internal-facing, not customer-facing — there is no external message here. The operating principle carried through every field: capture only what is needed to log an enquiry, log every one of them, and tag its source every time.

Scope of work

Assets and inputs supplied

Stage names and definitions from customer-journey.html and messaging-by-stage.html; the segment definitions from customer-profile.html (downsizer / estate-health-crisis / licence-loss / other); the same-business-day response SLA hypothesis, to confirm with Adam.

Constraints

No specific platform is named here — the choice is between a well-structured spreadsheet and a low-cost simple CRM tool, decided once Adam confirms budget appetite; simplicity of daily use matters more than feature completeness. No AI-powered lead scoring, no points-based B2B-style scoring model, no accounting-system or customer-support-platform integration — none is justified at this scale. Data handling (retention period, storage security standard, data-subject-access-request process) is flagged for direct legal-compliance-advisor input, not invented in this brief — the only firm rule here is: capture no more fields than listed above, and store the log somewhere access-controlled. Role-based access is moot with one person; both Adam and a second buyer get full access to the same single log once one joins.

Success and reporting

Year-one completeness target: 100% of enquiries logged with at minimum name, car details and source. No duplicate-rate or field-completeness benchmark is set beyond that — those become meaningful only after a full quarter of real logging. Weekly informal check (Now stage, habit-forming); monthly report covering lead volume, source split, conversion rates and Adam-hours per deal; quarterly full review. Every report folds into the same monthly structure as every other channel — no separate CRM dashboard.

First two weeks

  1. Stand up the four-entity lead log as a spreadsheet or simple tool — no sync complexity at launch.
  2. Agree the three-question qualification checklist wording with Adam.
  3. Start logging every enquiry from day one, including a short retrospective capture of the last month or two of settled sales.
  4. Confirm the same-business-day follow-up SLA with Adam.
  5. Set the weekly informal check-in habit for the first month.
