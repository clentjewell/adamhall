Messaging & Offer Architecture

How to read this document

This turns the position claimed in positioning.html into words customers hear (the message house) and things they can buy (the offer ladder). The brand platform behind both lives in brand-strategy.html. Everything here should be usable by a copywriter or a salesperson without needing to ask a follow-up question — where something is not yet safe to promise, that is flagged explicitly rather than smoothed over.

Message house

Core promise

"We'll come to you, tell you honestly what your car is worth, and if you want to sell, you'll have the money in your account within a day — no pressure, no strangers, no hassle." This is the confirmed promise already stated in offer-worksheet.html; every pillar below exists to support it, and no pillar should ever be marketed in a way that contradicts it.

Tone rules

The brand always explains before it declares — a number, a "no", or a piece of paperwork is never handed down without a plain-language reason attached. It always leaves room for "no" — every valuation is genuinely obligation-free, and copy should never create a sense that calling or accepting a visit is itself a commitment to sell. It never uses pressure, urgency or scarcity language that isn't true — the business's real constraint is capacity, not manufactured scarcity, and it should never borrow the "act now" tone of the price-led competitors it is positioned against (offer-worksheet.html, "Urgency & Scarcity"). It never uses haggling, negotiation or trade-in language — this is explicit brand voice guidance carried from Discover (USER.html, differentiation-opportunities.html). The tell that copy is off-voice: if a line could just as easily appear on CarBuyers' or We Buy Cars' website, it's wrong for this brand — the whole point is that no competitor in this category can honestly say what this brand says.

Offer architecture

Today there is effectively one offer in market: the free home-visit valuation leading, if the seller agrees, to an immediate purchase (offer-worksheet.html). The three-rung ladder below is the hypothesis already proposed internally and flagged for testing with Adam — it names and sharpens what mostly already happens, rather than inventing new products. None of this should be built or communicated to customers as a formal, named tier structure until confirmed with Adam (pricing-packaging-notes.html).

RungWhat it isWhat it costs the customerPain it relievesWhat it's for, strategically
Entry offer The free, obligation-free home-visit valuation. A phone call and a short home visit; no money, no commitment. Removes the biggest first-contact fear — "am I obligated to sell once I get a number?" (buying-triggers-barriers.html, objection 2). Trust-building and proof: this is the moment the "known person, not a stranger" pillar is either confirmed or lost in the seller's mind.
Core offer The direct purchase itself — Adam buys the car outright, pays generally within 24 hours via OSKO, arranges free pick-up. Their car, at the agreed number; no advertising, negotiating or paperwork burden. Answers the full pain stack at once: being ripped off, the private-sale slog, paperwork anxiety, and (for the estate/health-driven segment) the emotional weight of the task (customer-pain-points.html). The commercial spine of the business — carries the ~$4,200 average retail gross per car across the ~90% retail / 10% wholesale split (offer-worksheet.html, pricing-packaging-notes.html).
Expansion offer 1 — the estate/health-crisis service track A specifically named, gently marketed pathway for a family member selling a parent's or partner's car, using language and process built for that moment (differentiation-opportunities.html). The same core offer, delivered with deliberately adapted tone and pacing — not a separate price or product. Matches Job 3, "taking one thing off the list", in jobs-to-be-done.html — currently served well in practice but not named or targeted as its own pathway. Converts the highest-advocacy segment (customer-segments.html) into deliberate referral generation, not just a one-off sale — get the tone right here and it compounds into referral for the other segments.
Expansion offer 2 — the referral ask A systemised, non-digital way for a happy past customer to pass Adam's name on — not a "share this link" prompt (market-gaps.html gap 7). A small, natural ask at the point of highest satisfaction (payment day or pick-up), costing the customer only a moment. Answers a currently invisible gap — word of mouth happens informally today, with no confirmed mechanism (market-gaps.html, core-questions-digest.html CQ07 and CQ16). The long-term LTV engine flagged in offer-worksheet.html's proposed offer structure — currently a trap if promised as "working" before it exists (market-gaps.html).

The handoffs

  • Entry → Core. Trigger: the seller has heard the number and understood there's no obligation to accept it. Line: "That's the number — take your time, there's no pressure either way. If you'd like to go ahead, I can arrange payment and pick-up right now." An offer with no clear point where "no obligation" turns into "would you like to proceed" leaves the seller unsure whether they're allowed to say no — this handoff exists specifically to make that permission explicit.
  • Core → Expansion offer 1 (estate/health-crisis track). Trigger: the intake conversation reveals the seller is acting on behalf of someone else (a parent, a partner) rather than selling their own car. Line: "I understand you're doing this for your [relative] — I'll make sure everything's explained clearly so you both feel comfortable with it, at whatever pace works." [Owner-ready move, not yet built: language should ideally be drawn from Adam's own words about how he currently handles these situations — see CQ13, differentiation-opportunities.html.]
  • Core → Expansion offer 2 (referral ask). Trigger: payment has cleared and the seller has expressed satisfaction (verbally, or via a testimonial). Line: "If you know anyone else thinking about selling, I'd genuinely appreciate you passing my name on — that's how most of my customers find me." Currently a dead end without this handoff — market-gaps.html confirms the referral mechanism is unconfirmed and informal today (pending CQ07, CQ16); this line is a starting hypothesis for Adam to confirm or replace with his own words, not a finished script.

Flag: the referral ask currently has no confirmed handoff at all in the live business — this is the one rung on the ladder genuinely at risk of being a dead end until CQ07 and CQ16 are answered directly with Adam. It should not be marketed as a "referral program" until the ask itself is confirmed and tested in his own words.
