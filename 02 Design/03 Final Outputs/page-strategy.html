Page Strategy

How to read this document

This is the per-page content plan a copywriter can write from and a builder can build from, without a clarifying question. It covers the home page plus the seven pages that matter most from the full tree in sitemap.html: How It Works is deliberately excluded here as a lower-stakes, largely mechanical page once website-strategy.html's user flows are read; the three regional pages are covered once, in full, via the Northern Rivers page below, since Gold Coast and Brisbane follow the identical section pattern with area-specific proof swapped in — never a doorway stamp-out of the same copy. Every plan below is written for the personas in customer-profile.html and the stages in customer-journey.html; the words themselves are directional, not final copy — final wording is copy-deck.html's job.

Home

Single job. Confirm within one screen that this is a real, known, trustworthy individual — not a company — so a radio-primed visitor calls immediately and a cold visitor stays long enough to be convinced.

Visitor state arriving. Mixed. A majority arrive already knowing Adam's name from radio or word of mouth, with low awareness of alternatives and high pre-existing trust. A minority arrive cold from search or an AI assistant with zero context and need the page itself to do the convincing.

Section-by-section content plan.

  1. Hero — names Adam personally and the core promise (a real person comes to you and tells you honestly what your car is worth), phone number prominent, one line of geography (Gold Coast, Brisbane, Northern Rivers).
  2. Immediate proof — the 27-years and 4CRB radio-credibility line directly beneath the hero, since recognition is the single highest-leverage moment at this stage per messaging-by-stage.html.
  3. How it works, condensed to three or four steps (call, home visit, decide, paid), linking through to the full How It Works page.
  4. Testimonial strip — two or three short quotes spanning segments, including the Currumbin Waters estate quote, linking to the full Testimonials page.
  5. Segment/region signpost — a short, plain module pointing a cold visitor to Selling a Parent's or Family Member's Car and the three regional pages, so she self-selects rather than reading everything.
  6. FAQ teaser — the three most common objections (free? obligated? will my car be wanted?), each answered in one line, linking to the full FAQ.
  7. Close — the phone number repeated, plus the callback-request CTA for anyone not ready to call.

Conversion action. Primary: call the persistent number, repeated at the hero and the close. Secondary fallback: "Request a callback" for visitors not ready to call immediately.

Sell Your Car (hub)

Single job. State the entire offer — process, what's included, what's excluded — clearly enough that no regional page ever needs to explain it twice.

Visitor state arriving. Has already recognised the name (home page, radio or word of mouth) and wants the specifics: what cars are wanted, what happens at the visit, how fast payment arrives.

Section-by-section content plan.

  1. Hero — restates the core promise with more detail than the home page's condensed version.
  2. What we buy — car types and condition range, plus the honest line that some cars aren't a fit and the seller is told upfront, never left waiting (messaging-offer-architecture.html tone rules).
  3. The process, in full — call, share car details, home visit and valuation, decide with genuinely no obligation, paperwork and payment (fast, plain language, without overstating the settlement mechanics still pending confirmation), free pick-up.
  4. Real Market Pricing explainer — why the number is explained rather than handed down, using "a number that's meant to hold" and "explained plainly," never an unqualified "guaranteed never to move" claim, per positioning.html's proof-pending flag.
  5. Regional signpost — links to the three regional pages for area-specific proof.
  6. Testimonial and FAQ links.

Conversion action. Primary: call. Secondary: a callback-request form embedded at the foot of the page.

Sell Your Car — Northern Rivers

Gold Coast and Brisbane follow this identical section pattern with their own area-specific proof and detail — this plan is written once, in full, for Northern Rivers, and must never be copy-pasted with only the place name swapped.

Single job. Prove, to a Northern Rivers seller specifically, that this isn't a Queensland-only service that will make them travel — claiming a coverage gap no researched competitor visibly serves.

Visitor state arriving. Likely searching directly for a local term because competitors don't visibly serve the area; moderate awareness of the category, low awareness of Adam specifically unless referred.

Section-by-section content plan.

  1. Hero — names the region explicitly and states plainly that Adam visits Northern Rivers homes directly, with no need to travel to a Queensland branch — a direct, evidenced contrast with the branch-only, Queensland-only model of at least one named competitor.
  2. Local proof — testimonials from genuinely Northern Rivers customers, used here specifically rather than scattered generically, so the local claim is credible rather than merely asserted.
  3. What's different about a visit out this way — any honest note on scheduling; flag: travel logistics and any response-time claim are not documented in source material and must be confirmed with Adam before publishing — do not assert an SLA that isn't evidenced.
  4. The same process, restated briefly with a link back to the Sell Your Car hub for full detail — never duplicating the whole offer explanation, per the standards note against doorway-page stamp-outs.
  5. FAQ and testimonial links.

Conversion action. Primary: call. Secondary: the callback form, with a suburb field that's genuinely useful here to confirm reach.

Selling a Parent's or Family Member's Car

Single job. Tell the visitor, in the first two sentences, that this won't feel transactional — before she reads anything else.

Visitor state arriving. Cold or near-cold, since this segment typically has little prior brand awareness; high emotional load and time pressure without wanting to feel rushed; actively evaluating whether this feels safe versus generic.

Section-by-section content plan.

  1. Hero — names the situation directly (selling a parent's, partner's or relative's car) and states the tone promise up front; flag: the "same care Adam would want for his own family" framing in messaging-offer-architecture.html is owner-ready but not yet confirmed in Adam's own words — use with that caveat until CQ13 is answered.
  2. What's different about this path — no assumed requirement for the parent or relative to be present for every step; clear, simple next steps stated up front; unhurried follow-up.
  3. Proof — the Currumbin Waters testimonial in full, plus a short explanation of why this situation gets particular care.
  4. Situation-specific FAQ — does my parent need to be there, will there be pressure to decide quickly, how many visits does it take, is the price fair given I'm deciding on their behalf.
  5. Close — reassurance plus both conversion options.

Conversion action. Primary: the callback-request form, weighted first on this page since the visitor may not be free to talk right now. Secondary: a phone call, stated as equally welcome any time.

What's Your Car Worth?

Single job. Convert the 4CRB radio relationship from a background fact into a public, browsable proof asset and content engine.

Visitor state arriving. Two states — an existing radio listener confirming Adam is "the same fellow," and a cold organic or AI-assistant visitor who found this page through a valuation-related search with no radio context at all.

Section-by-section content plan.

  1. Hero — explains the show plainly for someone who's never heard of it: what it is, since 2010, on 4CRB 89.3FM, over 10,000 live on-air valuations.
  2. Why it matters as proof — the same approach used on air is the one applied to your car in person, at home; draws the credibility line explicitly.
  3. Content module — general, honest, non-HIGH-CONTROL car-valuation education (what affects a car's value, in plain terms); specific valuation rules or figures are out of scope here, pending CQ01–CQ03.
  4. Listen/tune-in details for the live show, carried over from the current site's Listen page.
  5. Links to Testimonials and Sell Your Car.

Conversion action. Primary: call, present but secondary to the credibility-building content itself — this page's real job is trust-building, per the "no CTA, deliberately" logic at the Exposure stage in messaging-by-stage.html, softened slightly since this is a page rather than a passive radio segment.

Testimonials

Single job. Give any visitor, regardless of which page they entered on, one place to independently verify "is this actually as good as it sounds."

Visitor state arriving. Already interested but seeking third-party confirmation before calling — the Consideration stage in customer-journey.html.

Section-by-section content plan.

  1. Intro — one line framing these as real, named customers across the whole service area.
  2. Testimonials organised by situation, not chronology — a downsizer group, an estate/health-crisis group leading with Currumbin Waters, and any licence-loss-adjacent examples — so a visitor in Jen's situation finds her mirror quickly rather than scrolling generic praise.
  3. A note on how to leave a review — a concrete, low-cost step toward closing the independent-review-volume gap named in positioning.html's proof-points table.
  4. Links back to the relevant segment and regional pages.

Conversion action. Primary: call. Secondary: callback form, both restated at the foot of the page since this is typically a mid-journey stop rather than an entry point.

FAQ

Single job. Answer, in the visitor's own words, every objection this audience is too polite or too unsure to ask aloud on the phone.

Visitor state arriving. Interested, most of the way convinced, doing due diligence before making the first call.

Section-by-section content plan.

  1. Core objections first — is the valuation really free, am I obligated to sell, will my car (older, high-kilometre, unusual) even be wanted.
  2. Estate/health-crisis-specific questions, linked from and to the family-car-sale page.
  3. Practical questions — how fast is payment, what paperwork is needed, is pick-up really free, do I need to prepare the car.
  4. A plain statement of what happens if the answer is "no, this car isn't a fit" — honesty that builds trust rather than just selling.

Conversion action. Primary: call. Every answer ends with an implicit permission to call rather than a pushy CTA, consistent with the brand's tone rule that it always leaves room for "no" (messaging-offer-architecture.html).

Contact / Request a Callback

Single job. Be the fastest possible path to a phone call or a logged callback for anyone who hasn't already converted from a persistent CTA elsewhere on the site.

Visitor state arriving. Ready or very nearly ready — this is a deliberately low-traffic page, since most conversions should happen from the persistent CTA on every other page; visitors who land here are actively looking for the number or a way to leave their details.

Section-by-section content plan.

  1. Phone number, large, first on the page, with the hours or availability Adam is happy to state.
  2. Callback-request form — name, phone, suburb, best time to call, optional car details — five fields, no more.
  3. Service-area confirmation (Gold Coast, Brisbane, Northern Rivers) so a visitor outside it isn't left guessing.
  4. A short reassurance line — no obligation, no pressure, calling costs nothing.

Conversion action. Primary: call. Secondary: the callback form itself, logged into the lead log recommended in measurement-plan.html — this page is where the business's lead-source and lead-volume tracking is most directly instrumented.
