Measurement Plan

How to read this document

This is the metric tree the whole engagement is scored on, and the document Deploy-phase reporting runs on. It exists because funnel-review.html confirms plainly that nothing about Adam Hall Buy My Car is measured today — no lead volume, no source split, no conversion rate, no Adam-hours per deal. Objective 1 in objectives-key-results.html ("make the business's engine visible") is deliberately the first objective in the whole engagement for exactly this reason: no other objective's key results can be judged against a baseline until this one is live. Every metric below traces up to one of the four objectives in objectives-key-results.html; orphan metrics that don't roll up to an objective are deliberately excluded.

The metric tree

Objective 1 — Make the business's engine visible

  • Business metrics: lead volume; lead source split; lead-to-visit conversion rate; visit-to-sale conversion rate; Adam-hours per deal; days lead-to-settlement; referral rate and referral source.
  • Channel-level metrics rolling up to lead source split: radio-attributed leads (4CRB); word-of-mouth/referral leads; website leads; other/unclassified leads.

Objective 2 — Turn Adam's judgement into a documented, transferable operating system

  • Business metrics: CORE questions answered and confirmed (count out of 22); payment-to-settlement sequence documented and confirmed (binary, HIGH-CONTROL); second-buyer identification and onboarding milestones reached.

Objective 3 — Grow revenue from ~$5M toward $7M without breaking the trust model

  • Business metrics: retail volume per month; retail/wholesale split; average retail gross per car; estate/health-crisis and Northern Rivers segment share of monthly volume; "one number, never moved" guarantee live and honoured (binary, gated).

Objective 4 — Build discovery and trust assets that don't depend on Adam being in the room

  • Business metrics: radio-and-referral proof engine formalised as a public asset (binary/milestone); new website live and structured for AI-chatbot and generative-search visibility (milestone); estate & health-crisis service track named and piloted in messaging (binary).

How channel numbers roll up: every lead captured by source (radio, word of mouth, website, other) rolls up into the lead source split under Objective 1, which in turn is the evidence base for whether Objective 4's new discoverability investment (the rebuilt website) is actually contributing new leads, and for whether Objective 3's segment-share key results are moving.

Reporting cadence

  • Weekly (informal, Now stage only). A quick check that the lead log is being completed consistently — this is habit-forming, not a formal report, and stops being necessary once the habit is proven.
  • Monthly. The four core Objective 1 metrics (lead volume, source split, conversion rates, Adam-hours per deal), plus retail volume and average retail gross. This monthly report is what checks the Now → Next advancement trigger in growth-roadmap.html — specifically, whether at least one full month of usable lead-log data exists.
  • Quarterly. Full review against the objectives in objectives-key-results.html, tied to progress toward the $7M target and the longer 10x/Cars4Us benchmark named in success-definition.html. This is also where the Next → Later advancement trigger is checked — whether the second buyer is handling a defined share of visits, whether valuation rules are consistent enough to sustain a public guarantee, and whether referral is generating a measured, named share of leads.
  • Phase gates (Discover → Design → Deploy). CORE completion and HIGH-CONTROL documentation status are reviewed at every gate, per Objective 2's own review cadence in objectives-key-results.html.
