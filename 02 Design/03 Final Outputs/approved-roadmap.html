Approved Roadmap

The Commitment

In Deploy, Maxxim will build the instrumentation the business has never had — a lead log, a rebuilt phone-first website, and a measured referral system — then bring 4CRB radio, community and print activity fully to life around it, holding paid Facebook and Google in reserve until the engine is provably working. The target is the growth path set out in success-definition.html: revenue moving from roughly $5M toward a $7M target (timeframe still TBD, an 18-month path is proposed), on a 10x trajectory benchmarked against Cars4Us's growth to acquisition. Nothing in this roadmap asks Adam to generate more demand before capacity is proven and measured.

Sequenced Deploy Work

  1. CRM lead log (Month 1). The precondition every later workstream depends on — traced to crm-plan.html and the "Now" stage of growth-roadmap.html. Runs first because nothing else can be measured until it exists.
  2. Website rebuild (Months 1–3). Phone-first, analytics and schema from day one — traced to website-strategy.html and website-brief.html. Runs in parallel with the lead log because it is the only channel not gated by an open client question.
  3. Creative production — logo, brand collateral, photo/video (Months 1–3, gated only on logo sign-off). Traced to design-brief.html, logo-brief.html, photo-video-brief.html. Sequenced to run independent of the CRM and website tracks, so creative isn't held hostage to instrumentation delays.
  4. Campaign 1 — "Prove the Engine" (Months 1–3). Instrumentation only, traced to campaign-calendar.html Q1 (Aug–Oct 2026) and marketing-plan.html. Runs before any paid or new-demand activity by design — the whole roadmap's ordering logic is: prove the number first, then scale it.
  5. SEO technical foundation (Months 2–4). Rides on the website build shipping — traced to seo-strategy.html's 3-month milestone. Sequenced after the site because schema and analytics can't be verified on a page that doesn't exist yet.
  6. Referral systemisation and EDM settlement automation (Months 3–6). Traced to partnership-referral-plan.html and edm-plan.html, gated on CQ07/CQ16 wording and the website's callback form/call tracking going live. Sequenced here because referral is the cheapest lever and should be systemised before any paid spend is considered.
  7. Campaign 2 — "Handled With Care" (Months 4–9). Estate/health-crisis content pilot and first PR/community-talk activity — traced to campaign-calendar.html Q2 and content-strategy.html Pillar 4, gated on CQ13.
  8. Campaign 3 — "We Come to the Rivers" (Months 6–12). Northern Rivers regional push, retirement-village partnerships, first print advertorial — traced to campaign-calendar.html Q3 and traditional-media-plan.html.
  9. Paid media activation — Facebook and Google Search (from Month 3 onward, condition-gated, not date-gated). Traced to paid-media-plan.html's Now→Next trigger: one month of usable lead-log data, plus CQ11/CQ06/CQ08 answered, plus the second-buyer conversation underway. Sequenced last among active channels because it is the only spend this roadmap has not already earned the right to make.
  10. Campaign 4 — "One Number, Never Moved" (gated, unscheduled). Traced to strategic-priorities.html Bet 4 and marketing-plan.html. Held outside this roadmap's committed dates until CQ01–CQ04 are answered — this is a decision point, not a delay.

Review Checkpoints

  • Monthly informal review (from Month 1) — reviews raw lead-log data quality and volume against measurement-plan.html Objective 1; triggers a re-plan conversation if data isn't capturing cleanly.
  • Q1 phase-gate review (by 18 Oct 2026) — reviews the full Objective 1 baseline (lead volume, source split, conversion rate, Adam-hours per deal) and formally decides the paid-media Now→Next trigger; a re-plan is triggered if the baseline isn't captured or CQ11/CQ01–CQ04 remain unanswered.
  • Q2 phase-gate review (by 17 Jan 2027) — reviews referral systemisation uptake and the estate/health-crisis content pilot against measurement-plan.html Objective 2 targets.
  • Q3 phase-gate review (by 18 Apr 2027) — reviews Northern Rivers performance and the local print advertorial against Objective 3 targets (retail volume, average gross, segment share).
  • Q4 phase-gate review (by 18 Jul 2027) — reviews year-one performance against success-definition.html in full and makes the go/no-go decision on Campaign 4, the "one number, never moved" guarantee.

Not in the Roadmap

  • The inspection tool, the e-commerce marketplace and the AI portal — explicitly cut from this engagement's scope in priority-problems.html.
  • TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, X and Snapchat, on either a paid or organic basis.
  • Display, Performance Max, Shopping campaigns, YouTube ads and programmatic media.
  • TV, out-of-home and cinema advertising, and any second radio station.
  • Owned events, trade shows and webinars.
  • An online valuation calculator and a public pricing page.
  • A blog and an email newsletter, and any A/B testing in EDM, in year one.
  • The "one number, never moved" guarantee claim itself — Campaign 4 is gated and unscheduled; no date in this roadmap commits to it.
