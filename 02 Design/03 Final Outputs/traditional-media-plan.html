Traditional Media Plan

How to read this document

This is the traditional (non-digital) media plan for Adam Hall Buy My Car, and it is built around one fact that overrides the usual channel-selection exercise: this business already has a proven, decade-old traditional media asset in the 4CRB 89.3FM “What’s Your Car Worth?” segment (10,000+ live, on-air valuations delivered since 2010), running continuously with no gap to fill. The job of this plan is not to build a traditional media presence from nothing — it is to deepen and formalise the asset that already exists, and to evaluate a small number of realistic, low-cost print and community options that fit a 60–75+ audience, exactly as scoped for this plan.

Three ground rules, carried from marketing-plan.html and applied throughout. First, no rate, reach, frequency or CPM figure in this plan is invented. Every number that would normally come from a rate card, an OzTAM or GfK report, or a masthead’s media kit is explicitly flagged below as to be quoted or to be confirmed — this plan states structure, evaluation criteria and process instead of guessing at numbers no source in this workspace confirms. Second, budget is unconfirmed (CQ18, marketing-plan.html); this plan uses the same Conservative and Moderate scenarios defined there, and states plainly which activity belongs to which. Third, traditional media activity is sequenced behind the capacity-and-measurement-first rule set in marketing-plan.html — nothing here should be read as a demand-generation push during Campaign 1.

This plan builds on brand-strategy.html, positioning.html, customer-profile.html, content-strategy.html, competitor-analysis.html, partnership-referral-plan.html and campaign-calendar.html, and does not repeat the segment, channel-mix or budget-scenario detail already established there.

1. Traditional Media Objectives

Reach and frequency targets. No confirmed reach or frequency figure is set here, because no audience measurement data for the 4CRB relationship or any print outlet is held in this workspace. [Hypothesis — recommend requesting audience/reach data directly from 4CRB and any print outlet considered, rather than assuming a figure.] The realistic objective is qualitative and structural: keep the existing radio asset running unchanged and formalised as a public proof point, and test — not commit to — a small number of print/community placements.

Geographic coverage. Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers NSW, matching the business’s actual service area (positioning.html). No national, or Sydney/Melbourne-metro, coverage is relevant or proposed.

Flight timing and duration. Tied to the same twelve-month calendar as campaign-calendar.html: radio continues always-on and unchanged throughout; any print/community testing is deliberately light in Quarter 1 and only tested from Quarter 2 onward, in line with Campaign 1 being asset-formalisation, not demand generation.

Brand awareness lift targets. No baseline brand-awareness study exists for this business (positioning.html names the absence of independent review or awareness data as an open gap). A formal awareness-lift target is not set here; the working proxy is radio- and print-attributed leads captured in the lead log described in measurement-plan.html.

2. Channel Recommendations

Recommended: Radio. The single strongest traditional channel this business has, already proven over a decade, already trusted by the exact audience this business serves, and already running at effectively no incremental cost to build from scratch. See Section 4.

Recommended, narrow scope: Print. Seniors’-interest publications, community newsletters and local/regional press are a realistic fit for a 60–75+ audience that, per audience-teardown.html, engages with tangible, local, low-pressure media rather than digital-first channels. See Section 5.

Not recommended: Television. Cost and reach mismatch for a three-region, one-person-capacity-capped business (marketing-plan.html, “channels explicitly not recommended”). TV buying at any meaningful reach level in the Gold Coast/Brisbane market would require a budget and a lead volume this business is not seeking — growing demand ahead of the capacity and measurement work already in progress is itself named as a risk to avoid (business-plan.html). See Section 3.

Not recommended: Out-of-home (OOH). Broad-reach billboard, transit or digital OOH buying is built for mass-market awareness at a scale and cost this business has no reason to pursue; radio already delivers the awareness and credibility role at a fraction of the cost, with a decade of proof behind it (marketing-plan.html). See Section 6.

Not recommended: Cinema. No evidence exists that this audience is reached effectively through cinema advertising, and cinema buying is priced and packaged for a mass, younger-skewing audience that does not match this business’s customer profile (customer-profile.html). Cinema is not assessed further in this plan.

Any channel investment ahead of the measurement baseline already in place is explicitly named as a risk in marketing-plan.html — no channel decision in this plan should be read as final until the lead log confirms it is working.

3. TV Plan

Not recommended. Free-to-air and subscription TV both require a reach and frequency commitment, and an accompanying budget, that do not match a capacity-capped, three-region business converting roughly 25 cars a month on trust alone with no paid marketing history. The awareness and credibility role TV would typically serve is already filled, at far lower cost and with a decade of proven local trust, by the 4CRB radio relationship. No further TV detail is developed in this plan; this recommendation should be revisited only if the business's scale and capacity change materially (for example, once a fully trained second buyer meaningfully lifts the capacity ceiling).

4. Radio Plan — the anchor channel

Station selection and market. 4CRB 89.3FM, an existing community/talk-format station already reaching this business’s exact audience through the weekly “What’s Your Car Worth?” segment, running since 2010. No other station is recommended for consideration in this Design phase — expanding to a second station is a genuinely separate, unproven investment and is treated below as a lower-priority, hypothesis-stage option, not a current recommendation.

Format alignment with brand personality. A community/talk-radio format matches the warm, plain-spoken, unhurried tone-of-voice guide in brand-strategy.html directly — the segment is already, by its nature, first-person, conversational and trust-building, which is exactly the channel-specific tone brand-strategy.html names as “the warmest and most conversational the brand ever gets.”

Daypart strategy. The current segment’s daypart, exact air time and weekly duration are not recorded in the workspace materials reviewed for this plan. Open item: confirm the current daypart and any scheduling detail directly with Adam or the station producer before treating any daypart assumption as fact.

Spot length recommendations. Not applicable in the usual 15s/30s/60s sense — the existing arrangement is a recurring on-air segment/feature, not a purchased spot. If a produced sign-off line, promo or short paid spot is developed in future (most plausibly tied to the gated Campaign 4 guarantee launch in marketing-plan.html), standard Australian commercial radio spot lengths of 15, 30 or 60 seconds would apply — exact specification and production requirements should be confirmed directly with 4CRB at that time, not assumed here.

Sponsorship and integration opportunities to explore with 4CRB, once a formalised proof asset exists to co-promote (per partnership-referral-plan.html’s first outreach move):

  • A recurring sign-off or credit line explicitly citing the 10,000+ valuations milestone, once permission to use that figure publicly is confirmed.
  • Whether 4CRB has any on-demand, podcast or website replay capability for the segment, which would let content-strategy.html’s “Listen” page repurpose it further — this capability is not confirmed and should be asked about directly, not assumed.
  • An expanded segment cadence or a simple content collaboration (for example the station sharing a recap or testimonial on its own channels), as already proposed in partnership-referral-plan.html.

Estimated reach per market. Not stated. No audience or reach data for 4CRB is held anywhere in this workspace’s source material. Recommendation: request 4CRB’s own audience and reach figures directly as part of any deepened commercial conversation, and treat any figure supplied as station-sourced and to be verified, not as an independently confirmed number.

Budget allocation. The current arrangement’s commercial terms (if any change from the status quo, ongoing free/reciprocal arrangement, or paid arrangement) are not documented in this workspace — marketing spend actuals are an open CORE question (CQ18, marketing-plan.html). Any rate for an expanded or new radio arrangement is to be quoted directly by 4CRB; nothing in this plan should be read as an agreed or estimated cost for radio activity.

5. Print Plan

Recommended, in three deliberately narrow categories matched to how a 60–75+ audience across this business’s three regions actually reads local media (audience-teardown.html):

Seniors’ newspapers and publications

Publication selection. Australian seniors’-interest publications with a Queensland/northern-NSW readership — the same outlet types recommended for earned coverage in pr-plan.html (for example YourLifeChoices, Starts at 60, and any National Seniors Australia or COTA member communications, once each is confirmed to accept advertising or advertorial content). Exact current circulation and readership data for each is not held in this workspace and must be requested directly from each publication’s own media kit before any placement is confirmed — no circulation figure is stated here.

Community newsletters

Publication selection. Newsletters belonging to the same community organisations already targeted for speaking engagements and partnerships — Probus, CWA, Rotary, U3A, RSL sub-branches and retirement villages (per partnership-referral-plan.html). These typically carry little or no paid-placement cost when tied to an existing speaking or partnership relationship, functioning more as a goodwill mention than a media buy — this is a genuinely low-cost option, not one requiring a formal rate negotiation in most cases.

Local press

Publication selection. Community and regional papers matched to the business’s actual coverage area: News Corp’s Quest Newspapers community titles across Brisbane and Gold Coast suburbs for the Queensland side of the business, and Northern Rivers regional/community papers (titles covering the Lismore, Byron and Tweed areas) specifically for the Northern Rivers geography claim under Campaign 3 in marketing-plan.html. Exact current mastheads, sections and rates should be confirmed directly — regional media ownership and titles change, and none of the above should be treated as a verified, current media kit.

Format and positioning, across all three categories

Ad size recommendations. No specific size is committed here; a small display ad or classified/directory-style listing is the realistic minimum unit, but this plan recommends prioritising advertorial or sponsored content over a standard display ad wherever a publication offers it, since a trust-first, explain-don’t-declare brand voice (brand-strategy.html) reads far better as a short, genuine article than as a conventional ad unit. Advertorial copy can draw directly on the content pillars already built in content-strategy.html (particularly Pillar 1, “Meet Adam,” and Pillar 5, the Northern Rivers coverage story), so no separate advertorial content needs to be created from scratch.

Positioning preferences. No specific page position (right-hand page, editorial adjacency) is assumed; the general principle recommended is editorial-adjacent or advertorial placement over an intrusive ad unit, consistent with the brand voice above.

Frequency and insertion schedule. Deliberately modest and occasional rather than a recurring insertion schedule — matching the “a local paper advertorial, printed material for partner handoff” illustrative Moderate-scenario spend already named in marketing-plan.html’s budget allocation. A recurring schedule is not recommended until a first placement’s performance is confirmed through the lead log.

Budget allocation. All print rates are to be quoted directly by each masthead once specific candidates are shortlisted; this plan intentionally states no rate figure. Any confirmed print spend should sit inside the Moderate-scenario “local community/print-adjacent” budget line already described in marketing-plan.html (illustrative ~20% of any Moderate-scenario test budget), not as a new, separate line.

6. Out-of-Home Plan

Not recommended. Static or digital billboards, transit advertising and street-furniture formats are priced and built for broad, mass-audience reach across a metro area — a poor cost-fit for a three-region, capacity-capped business with no confirmed traditional-media budget. No format, location strategy or duration is developed here as a result.

One low-cost, local presence tactic sits outside the scope of a formal OOH plan but is worth noting for completeness: vehicle signage, already referenced as an existing or low-cost brand asset in brand-strategy.html’s logo usage principles (a logo that must work on printed flyers and vehicle signage as well as digital sizes). This is a one-off, low-cost local visibility tactic tied to the vehicle Adam already drives for home visits, not a paid OOH media buy, and is not assessed further here.

8. Creative Requirements

Radio. No new spot production is required for the existing segment format. If a produced sign-off line, credit or short promo is developed in future (most plausibly tied to a Campaign 4 guarantee launch), standard Australian commercial radio spot lengths (15s, 30s, 60s) and any file format, loudness or delivery requirements should be confirmed directly with 4CRB’s own production specifications at that time — none is assumed here.

Print. File format (typically a print-ready PDF, correct colour mode and bleed) and submission deadlines should be confirmed against each shortlisted masthead’s own advertising specifications and media kit; a typical Australian print lead time is in the order of 5–10 business days before an issue’s ad deadline, but this is a general industry norm to verify per masthead, not a confirmed deadline for any specific title named above.

Typography and legibility. Any print creative must prioritise large, highly legible type over trend-driven design choices, consistent with brand-strategy.html’s typography direction for a 60–75+ audience reading on a printed page or a phone screen.

Creative versioning needs. A single, consistent advertorial-style creative approach is recommended across print/community placements, drawing on the same content-pillar material used elsewhere (content-strategy.html), rather than producing separate, bespoke creative for each masthead — keeping production effort proportionate to this business’s solo-operator capacity.

No TV, OOH or cinema creative specifications are developed, consistent with those channels not being recommended (Sections 3 and 6).

9. Integration with Digital

Vanity URLs and QR codes. Any print/advertorial placement should carry a simple, memorable reference to the website (once rebuilt, per website-strategy.html) and, once the site exists, a QR code linking directly to the relevant page (for example the Northern Rivers area page referenced in content-strategy.html Pillar 5).

Social media handles and hashtags. Any print creative should carry the business’s Facebook Page reference, consistent with social-strategy.html.

Search term prompts. Radio sign-off content, once formalised, is a natural place for a simple “search for Adam Hall Buy My Car” prompt, supporting seo-strategy.html’s branded-search goals — this should be introduced only once the new website exists to receive that search traffic meaningfully.

Expected search lift from radio and print activity. Not stated as a figure — no data exists to support one. [Hypothesis.] This should be monitored, not assumed, once GA4-equivalent analytics are live as part of the website rebuild (Marketing Objective 5, marketing-plan.html), by watching for any branded-search or direct-traffic uplift coinciding with radio or print activity.

Multi-touch attribution approach. Given the absence of any CRM or advanced attribution tooling (marketing-plan.html), the realistic cross-channel measurement approach is the same self-reported “how did you hear about us” manual tagging at first contact used across every other channel in measurement-plan.html — not a modelled or probabilistic attribution system, which is not justified at this budget and data maturity.

Retargeting strategies triggered by traditional media exposure. Not recommended under the Conservative scenario, since no confirmed digital ad presence exists to retarget through. Only relevant if a Moderate-scenario paid-social or search test proceeds (see paid-media-plan.html, once commissioned) — and even then, should be scoped as a small, deliberate test, not a broad retargeting programme.

10. Measurement & Reporting

Radio. Self-reported “how did you hear about us” attribution, manually tagged at first contact in the lead log (per measurement-plan.html). No syndicated audience-measurement data (of the kind OzTAM provides for television) is held for this specific 4CRB relationship in this workspace; if 4CRB can supply its own audience or reach data as part of a deepened commercial conversation, that should be requested and treated as station-sourced, not independently verified here.

Print. Circulation and readership audit data should be requested directly from each masthead as part of confirming any placement; no circulation figure is assumed or stated in this plan.

Brand lift study. Not recommended at this budget and scale. A formal pre/post brand-lift study (awareness, consideration, preference) is a tool suited to budgets well above what either the Conservative or Moderate scenario in marketing-plan.html describes; the realistic proxy for this business is the radio- and print-attributed lead share tracked through the lead log, reviewed monthly and quarterly per measurement-plan.html.

Media mix modelling. Explicitly out of scope. This technique is generally suited to campaigns above roughly $100,000 in spend; nothing in either budget scenario for this business approaches that threshold, and this plan does not recommend building toward it.

ROI framework. No cost-per-thousand (CPM), cost-per-reach-point, or estimated cost-per-acquisition figure is stated anywhere in this plan, because no rate, reach or frequency number has been confirmed for any channel described above. Every rate and reach figure in this plan is explicitly a to-be-quoted item, to be requested directly from 4CRB and from any shortlisted print masthead before a spend decision is made — not estimated or assumed here. Once real rates and the first quarter of lead-log data exist, a genuine cost-per-lead comparison across radio, print and every other channel in marketing-plan.html becomes possible.

Reporting cadence and milestone checkpoints. The same monthly and quarterly cadence as marketing-plan.html and measurement-plan.html — a monthly channel-level report and a quarterly strategic review — rather than a separate traditional-media-specific reporting cycle.
