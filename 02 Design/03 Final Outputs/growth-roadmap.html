Growth Roadmap

How to read this document

This is the staged path from where Adam Hall Buy My Car stands today — ~$5M revenue, ~25 cars/month, one person running the entire operating system — toward the $7M near-term target and the longer 10x path benchmarked against Cars4Us (founded 2019, sold to Toyota Tsusho for a reported $120M in 2026, per competitor-analysis.html and success-definition.html). The four bets in strategic-priorities.html are staged here into Now / Next / Later horizons. Metric definitions, baselines and instrumentation live in measurement-plan.html — this document references them, it does not redefine them.

Now (0–3 months, aligned to Design-phase kick-off)

Growth levers active. Capacity preparation and measurement instrumentation — deliberately not demand generation. This stage exists to make growth possible and measurable before it is pursued.

What the priorities contribute. Bet 1 (strategic-priorities.html) keeps CORE mapping running at its existing twice-weekly cadence, prioritising CQ11, CQ06 and CQ08, and opens the second-buyer conversation. Bet 2 puts a lead log and source-tagging discipline live from day one — the precondition for every later measurement in this roadmap. Bet 3's scaffolding (a referral-source field) starts here even though the tested script itself waits on a CORE answer. Bet 4's proof-asset formalisation begins with securing Adam's written permission to use existing testimonials and the 10,000+ valuations figure.

What "done" means for this stage. A lead log and source tagging running for at least one full month; CQ11, CQ06 and CQ08 answered and confirmed in writing; a second-buyer candidate conversation under way; written permission secured for the proof-engine claim; a referral-ask script drafted (not yet published).

Next (3–9 months)

Growth levers active. Market penetration via capacity (a second buyer trained and operating), combined with market development into the two segments and one geography this business already touches but has never formally claimed: estate/health-crisis sellers and Northern Rivers NSW.

What the priorities contribute. Bet 1 continues into second-buyer onboarding against the documented Adam Hall Standard. Bet 3's tested referral ask goes live and is tracked from month 3. Bet 4 formalises the proof engine publicly and pilots estate/health-crisis messaging, alongside the new website (Objective 4 in objectives-key-results.html) built to be visible to AI chatbots and generative search, replacing the current five-page brochure site.

What "done" means for this stage. A second buyer handling a defined, named share of visits to the Adam Hall Standard; referral generating a measured, named share of monthly leads; the new website live; estate/health-crisis and Northern Rivers segments each contributing a measurable, named share of monthly volume (Objective 3, key result 2).

Later (9–18+ months, toward the $7M and 10x path)

Growth levers active. Completion of market development, plus the product-development moves that depend entirely on Next-stage documentation being consistent and proven: a formalised, publicly claimed "one number, never moved" guarantee, and the Documented Operating System presented as a public trust artefact, not just an internal SOP. The e-commerce marketplace website, the vehicle inspection/input tool and the AI "ask it anything" operating-system portal sit beyond this stage as confirmed future or separately priced scope (phase1-work-digest.html) — named here as the eventual diversification horizon toward the 10x/Cars4Us comparable, not a deliverable of this roadmap.

What the priorities contribute. Bet 4's strongest, HIGH-CONTROL-adjacent claim launches only once Bet 1's valuation-rule documentation is consistent enough to make the promise safely every time. A numeric definition of "sellable" (CQ08) is adopted as the north-star key result for the multi-year path, benchmarked against Cars4Us's roughly seven-year build-to-sale timeline (2019–2026).

What "done" means for this stage. Revenue at or approaching $7M without an increase in Adam's personal hours per car; the "one number, never moved" guarantee live and provably honoured; the numeric sale-readiness definition confirmed and tracked.

Dependencies between stages

Next assumes Now delivered a working measurement habit and enough CORE documentation to define what "to the Adam Hall Standard" actually means — a second buyer cannot be trained against a standard that doesn't yet exist in writing. This is the most fragile link in the roadmap: if CORE mapping stalls, the entire Next stage stalls with it, because capacity growth, referral systemisation and any public trust claim all trace back to the same documentation work.

Later's flagship claim — the "one number, never moved" guarantee — depends entirely on Next's valuation-rule documentation being not just written but consistent in practice. This is the second fragile link: publishing the guarantee before it is provably true would do more reputational damage than never making the claim at all (differentiation-opportunities.html), so this stage gate must not be shortcut for a marketing deadline.

Where current capacity caps growth. Throughout Now and into Next, the business remains capped at roughly 25 cars a month regardless of demand, because every lead, valuation, decision and settlement still routes through one person's calendar (funnel-review.html). This ceiling does not lift until a second buyer is trained and handling visits unsupervised — the single event this entire roadmap is sequenced around.

Advancement triggers

Triggers are conditions, not calendar dates — a date alone is a deadline, not a trigger. Full metric definitions are in measurement-plan.html.

  • Now → Next. Advances when: the lead log and source tagging have run for at least one full month with usable data; CQ11, CQ06 and CQ08 are answered and confirmed in writing; and a second-buyer candidate has been identified and an onboarding plan agreed. All three conditions, not a date, must be true together.
  • Next → Later. Advances when: the second buyer is handling a defined, named share of visits unsupervised to the Adam Hall Standard; the valuation and settlement rules are documented and consistent enough to sustain a public "one number, never moved" claim without exception; and referral is generating a measured, named share of monthly leads. Only once all three hold does the flagship guarantee and the public Operating System artefact become safe to launch.
