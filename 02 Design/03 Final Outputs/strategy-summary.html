Strategy Summary

Strategic Direction

  • We build capacity before we buy demand. The business already converts around 25 cars a month on trust alone with zero paid history — the constraint is that the whole engine lives in one person's head, so Design instruments Adam's judgement and measures the funnel before a single incremental lead is sought (strategic-priorities.html, growth-roadmap.html).
  • We claim the named-person trust position, not a company position. Every named competitor is a company, a branch network or a dealer panel; Adam is a person who attends every visit himself — that gap is the whole strategy (positioning.html, brand-strategy.html).
  • We systemise the referral before we pay for reach. Referral already works organically — "I will only sell my cars to Adam" (Rex, Kyogle) — and is the cheapest volume lever available (partnership-referral-plan.html).
  • We hold the brand-naming decision open, deliberately. Scenario C keeps "Adam Hall Buy My Car" live today while building "The Adam Hall Standard" as a documented, transferable sub-brand, deferring CQ11 to a real trigger rather than forcing it now (brand-strategy.html, logo-brief.html).
  • We rebuild the website as the instrumentation backbone, not a brochure. Phone call first, callback form second, no calculator — because a valuation is a home-visit judgement, not an algorithm (website-strategy.html).

Strategy summaries

Brand

Positioning: a trust-and-ease-maximising, fully personal way to sell a car for sellers 60–75+ across the Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers — the same known person handling the whole thing, at your home, backed by 27+ years in the trade and a decade of public, on-air valuation credibility. Archetype: The Caregiver (primary), The Everyman (secondary). Differentiator: named-person continuity plus an unreplicable radio proof engine. Visual/verbal direction: Harbour Navy and Brass Trust palette (deliberately avoiding the category's red/yellow "sale" tones), Lora/PT Sans type, tone Honest, Calm and Direct. See brand-strategy.html, positioning.html.

Customer

Primary persona: Barbara, 71, a downsizer whose decision order is trust, then a fair explained number, then minimal effort and strangers, then payment speed, then price. Key insight: listing, photographing and fielding calls from strangers costs more effort than the money is worth. Biggest opportunity: the estate/health-crisis segment (Jen, 46) — a confirmed market gap with no competitor content addressing it. See customer-profile.html.

Business

Top three priorities: document Adam's judgement into a transferable operating system; instrument the business before optimising it; systemise the referral ask. Growth lever: market penetration, not demand generation — deliberately, because the constraint is capacity, not demand. Primary risk: key-person dependency, and publishing an unproven "one number, never moved" guarantee before it is provably true. See business-plan.html, strategic-priorities.html.

Marketing

Channel system: 4CRB radio anchor, referral/partnership, Facebook and Google (gated), community events and seniors' print, all converging on the phone call. Four campaigns in sequence: Prove the Engine (instrumentation only) → Handled With Care → We Come to the Rivers → One Number, Never Moved (gated, unscheduled). Budget: no confirmed figure — CQ18 is open — every plan is scenario-scaled between Conservative (near-zero net-new spend) and Moderate (illustrative percentage split only). See marketing-plan.html.

Copy & messaging

Core message: "We'll come to you, tell you honestly what your car is worth, and if you want to sell, you'll have the money in your account within a day — no pressure, no strangers, no hassle." Value proposition: a free home-visit valuation, payment within about 24 hours, free pick-up. Tone: honest, calm, warm. See copy-deck.html, messaging-offer-architecture.html.

Website

Sitemap decisions: 15 pages, flat structure, one level deep, with Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers regional pages nested under a single Sell Your Car hub; no valuation calculator, no public pricing page and no blog. Primary conversion flow: persistent phone link → home visit; the 5-field callback form is secondary. Technical approach: full rebuild on a static site (Astro-based), Cloudflare Pages, WCAG 2.1 AA with large type for the 60–75+ audience. See website-strategy.html, sitemap.html.

Social

Platform selection: Facebook only — both target personas are there; YouTube is a passive video library; Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn and X are explicitly skipped. Five content pillars, led by "A Person, Not a Company" and "Relief, Not Another Task." Posting approach: 2 posts a week conservative, rising to 3–4 once support capacity exists; paid social is $0 until a measurement baseline exists. See social-strategy.html.
