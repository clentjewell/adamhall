Events Brief

The job

Build a light-touch community events presence, distilled from events-plan.html, that costs Adam no more than a handful of hours a month. No events run in Quarter 1 (August–October 2026) — that quarter is groundwork only, identifying and making first contact with candidate community organisations and a retirement village, consistent with Campaign 1 being asset-formalisation, not a demand push.

Audience

Downsizers reached through existing community organisations they already attend (Probus, CWA, Rotary, U3A, RSL sub-branches); and downsizers actively moving, reached via retirement village/downsizing-service touchpoints. A Northern Rivers-specific touchpoint follows once current Northern Rivers volume is confirmed with Adam.

The message

"What your car is really worth, and how to avoid the common traps when selling privately" — genuinely useful, non-salesy community content in Adam's own conversational, radio-honed voice. Never a hard close or a group sign-up pitch; no early-bird or urgency tactics of any kind.

Assets and inputs supplied

partnership-referral-plan.html for the partner outreach already under way; content-strategy.html Pillars 1, 3 and 5 for talk content and recap material; the "Selling a parent's car" talk topic, gated behind Adam's own words on the estate/health-crisis situation being captured (CQ13).

Constraints

No owned, self-run public event; no large trade shows or multi-day expos requiring sustained booth staffing; no webinars or virtual events — none fit this audience or this business's one-person capacity. No more than one or two events active in any given quarter. Any expo booth should be staffed by Adam personally — if he cannot attend, do not proceed with that booth that period. No confirmed attendance or conversion figure exists for any event format — every number is a hypothesis pending real data. Budget: Conservative scenario is time only, no cash cost beyond an occasional small thank-you gesture or printed flyer; Moderate scenario adds a small, modest sponsorship or expo fee, sitting inside the existing local community/print-adjacent budget line, never a new category.

Success and reporting

At least one community speaking engagement delivered by month 4–6, tracked as a distinct lead-log source from that point. Inclusion in one retirement village's official resource list by month 6. One Northern Rivers community touchpoint by month 9. One recap content piece produced per event held. No lead-volume or revenue target is set for events — this is a trust and relationship channel first; any lead volume is measured and reported, not assumed in advance. Reported monthly, folded into the same report as every other channel; a full-year review in Q4 replaces this hypothesis-based calendar with an evidence-based one.

First two weeks

  1. Identify one to two candidate community organisations and one retirement village/downsizing service to approach, per partnership-referral-plan.html's first outreach moves.
  2. Draft the core talk content from content-strategy.html Pillars 1 and 3 — no new material needed from scratch.
  3. Agree with Adam the realistic monthly time allowance for events, so cadence never outpaces his home-visit capacity.
  4. Confirm who attends with Adam to handle photography and logistics, freeing him to focus only on speaking.
  5. Set up the lead-log source tag ("event — [organisation name]") ahead of the first talk.
