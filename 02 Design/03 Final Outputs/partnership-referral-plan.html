Partnership / Referral Plan

How to read this document

This is growth through other people's audiences — the channel marketing-plan.html names as the highest-leverage lever this business currently under-invests in, because the proven, organic referral behaviour already evident in testimonials ("I will only sell my cars to Adam" — Rex, Kyogle) has no formal ask, no tracking and no incentive behind it (funnel-review.html; Bet 3, strategic-priorities.html).

A compliance note that governs every recommendation below. This plan does not invent or assume any regulatory permission, endorsement, accreditation or referral-fee arrangement with a regulated professional (solicitor, financial planner, medical practitioner). Any partnership involving a legal, financial or health practitioner must be checked against that professional's own conduct rules before any incentive, commission or reciprocal-referral arrangement is proposed — this is a matter for Adam and his own advisers to confirm directly, not something Maxxim asserts as compliant here. Where a partner type is more hypothesis than evidenced opportunity, it is flagged as such rather than presented as a confirmed channel.

This document builds on the segment and journey work in customer-profile.html and customer-journey.html, and the untested partnership ideas already named in business-plan.html ("potential future referral relationships with allied-health, aged-care or estate-adjacent networks are untested ideas worth piloting carefully given the sensitivity involved") and audience-teardown.html (GP/allied-health as an inferred, unconfirmed referral channel for the licence-loss segment). Nothing here should be read as an existing relationship — every partner type below is a recommendation to pursue, not a confirmed channel already in place.

What each partnership looks like

4CRB 89.3FM

Offer to the partner. A decade of proven, valuable segment content and audience engagement; propose formalising or expanding the arrangement now that a documented "10,000+ valuations" proof asset exists to co-promote. Offer to their audience. Continued free, honest, entertaining car-valuation content delivered by someone they already trust — no change to what listeners already value.

Local seniors' community organisations

Offer to the partner. A genuinely useful, non-salesy guest talk — "what your car is really worth, and how to avoid the common traps when selling privately" — delivered as community value, not a pitch. Offer to their audience. Practical, trustworthy information from a known local figure, plus an easy, no-pressure way to get a free valuation if and when they need one.

Retirement villages and downsizing/move-coordination services

Offer to the partner. A reliable, no-pressure answer to a question their coordinators are asked constantly ("what do I do with the car?"), removing a friction point from their own move process at no cost to them. Offer to their audience. One less thing to organise during an already overwhelming move, handled by someone the village is comfortable recommending.

Solicitors and estate/probate practitioners

Offer to the partner. A dependable, respectful referral point for a small, practical estate task (the deceased's or incapacitated client's vehicle) that is otherwise a minor administrative headache for them and their clients — offered as a genuine service to their clients, not as a paid referral arrangement unless and until confirmed appropriate by both parties' own professional advice. Offer to their audience. A gentle, dignified, obligation-free way to deal with one item on an already difficult list, handled by someone experienced with exactly this kind of situation (content-strategy.html, Pillar 4).

Aged-care transition services and placement consultants

Offer to the partner. The same dependable, no-fee-implied referral point, positioned as a genuine resource for families they are already supporting through a hard transition. Offer to their audience. Relief from one more decision during a health- or care-driven move, handled with the same care the consultant's own service is built on.

Removalists and downsizing/decluttering services

Offer to the partner. A natural two-way referral relationship between local trades serving the same moment (a downsizing move) — offer to recommend them in return where appropriate. Offer to their audience. A convenient answer to "what do I do with the car while I'm sorting everything else out," from a business their removalist or declutterer already trusts enough to recommend.

Financial planners and accountants

Offer to the partner. A trustworthy referral point for a small, concrete part of a client's broader financial or estate picture — again, no fee-based arrangement assumed without independent confirmation it is appropriate. Offer to their audience. A fair, well-explained number for an asset that is often an afterthought in a bigger financial conversation.

GPs and allied health / driving-assessment services (hypothesis)

Offer to the partner. [Hypothesis, lowest priority] A gentle, non-clinical resource to mention only if a patient raises the topic themselves — never framed as medical advice or a clinical referral. Offer to their audience. A respectful, discreet way to handle a licence-loss situation without a drawn-out process, at a moment that is often already sensitive for the patient.

Referral mechanism for existing customers

The ask, in Adam's working words (drawn from messaging-by-stage.html, Stage 6 — to be tested and refined in Adam's own voice, not treated as a finished script until confirmed): "If this was a good experience, I'd really appreciate you telling someone else who might need the same thing. Know someone in a similar spot? Pass on my number." Flag: this is a starting hypothesis pending CQ07 and CQ16 — Adam's own wording should replace this once captured, per messaging-offer-architecture.html.

The incentive. The default recommendation is a social/relational incentive only — being the person who told a friend about a good, safe experience during a hard moment — rather than a financial incentive, which risks feeling transactional precisely in the estate/health-driven moments where trust matters most (conversion-pathway.html). A small financial thank-you is a testable idea worth raising with Adam directly, but it is not assumed here and should never be offered to a downsizer or estate/health-driven customer in a way that could read as a quid pro quo for the number they were paid.

The moment. Immediately after a well-handled sale — payment has cleared and the seller has expressed satisfaction, verbally or via a testimonial (customer-journey.html, Stage 6). This is deliberately the point of highest goodwill in the entire journey, and the ask should never be made earlier (e.g. during the home visit itself), where it could read as conditional on a "yes."

First three outreach moves

  1. Deepen the 4CRB relationship. Who: the station's producer or manager for "What's Your Car Worth?". Opening message angle: with a decade of proven audience value and a fresh set of formalised proof assets (testimonials, the 10,000+ valuations claim) now ready to co-promote, propose either an expanded segment cadence or a simple content collaboration (e.g. the station sharing a recap or testimonial on its own channels). A good first "yes" looks like: an agreed expansion of the existing arrangement, or a co-promoted piece of content — not a cold pitch, since this relationship already exists.
  2. A local seniors' organisation speaking slot. Who: the secretary or events coordinator of one Gold Coast or Brisbane Probus, CWA, Rotary or U3A chapter. Opening message angle: offer a free, genuinely useful talk — "what your car's really worth, and how to avoid the traps when selling privately" — framed entirely as community value, with no pitch embedded in the talk itself. A good first "yes" looks like: a booked speaking slot, ideally with access to a mailing list or newsletter mention for the FAQ/testimonial content this generates.
  3. One retirement village or downsizing-service partnership. Who: a lifestyle or move coordinator at a single Gold Coast or Brisbane retirement village, or an independent downsizing-concierge business. Opening message angle: become the go-to, no-pressure answer to "what do we do with the car" that comes up constantly during their residents' moves — a genuine service offer, not a paid placement pitch at this stage. A good first "yes" looks like: inclusion in the village's official "moving in/moving out" resource list or welcome pack.

All three moves are sequenced deliberately ahead of the higher-caution professional-referral types (solicitors, financial planners, health practitioners) — they require no compliance review, build on an existing or easily-earned trust relationship, and generate real-world evidence (a tested referral flow, a documented conversation, a proof point) that can inform how carefully to approach the more sensitive partner types later, once Bet 1's documentation work and Adam's own guidance are further along (strategic-priorities.html).
