SEO Strategy

How to read this document

This is the organic-search plan for the rebuilt site described in website-strategy.html, sitemap.html and page-strategy.html — kept consistent with those siblings' keyword mentions and priorities rather than re-deriving them. It also applies the Layer-1 on-page standards every Maxxim-built site ships (LocalBusiness JSON-LD, per-suburb pages, sitemap/robots/llms.txt, one NAP source, semantic headings, alt text) to this specific business. Evidence is drawn from competitor-analysis.html, market-gaps.html, website-crawl-digest.html and discovery-dataset.html.

No keyword-volume or rank-tracking data exists for this client yet — the current site has "no visible SEO/AI-search structuring beyond standard meta tags" (website-crawl-digest.html) and no analytics of any kind. Every keyword-difficulty rating and competitor-authority read below is a directional estimate from the live competitor research already conducted (20 July 2026), not a data-backed figure from a keyword-research or backlink tool — flagged throughout, with a recommendation to run a proper keyword and backlink audit once SEO tooling budget is confirmed. No milestone below promises a page-one ranking on a fixed date.

1. SEO objectives

Baseline. Zero. There is no existing keyword ranking, backlink or analytics baseline to grow from — month-1 targets are about instrumentation and indexation, not position.

3-month milestone. The rebuilt site live and indexed; a valid sitemap.xml submitted; LocalBusiness JSON-LD verified (for example via Google's Rich Results Test); Google Search Console and web analytics reporting; a ranking baseline established for the keyword set in Section 3 — not yet a page-one expectation.

6-month milestone. Page-one presence on the low-competition, high-intent local clusters where competition is genuinely thin — Northern Rivers regional terms and estate/health-crisis terms (both confirmed open gaps in market-gaps.html); organic-attributed calls and callback requests appearing, for the first time, in the monthly report.

12-month milestone. Building competitive visibility on the harder-contested Gold Coast and Brisbane "sell my car" head terms against CarBuyers' and carsales Instant Offer's mature SEO footprints; a public review presence established, closing the gap named in positioning.html's proof-points table; enough data to judge whether SEO is a meaningful lead-source contributor alongside radio and referral.

Tie to business KPIs. Organic sessions and rankings are leading indicators only. The metric that matters is website-attributed leads (calls plus callback requests) rolling into the same lead-source-split metric already defined in measurement-plan.html — SEO reporting should never be presented as traffic or ranking movement alone.

2. Technical SEO audit framework

Priority order below is by impact, not a flat checklist.

  1. Highest priority — LocalBusiness JSON-LD, sitemap.xml, robots.txt, canonical tags. Near-zero build cost, highest impact for a local service business with zero existing structuring; the single biggest win available (per the Maxxim Layer-1 SEO standard). Canonical tags matter specifically here given the near-duplicate structure of the three regional pages.
  2. Second — a full legacy-URL redirect map. The current five-page WordPress site (website-crawl-digest.html) presumably holds whatever organic and brand-search equity exists today (27-years, "Adam Hall" name searches) — every current URL must 301 to its new-site equivalent before the old site is retired, or that equity is lost outright.
  3. Third — page speed and Core Web Vitals. Achievable largely by default on the static rebuild recommended in website-strategy.html; the practical risk is unoptimised hero and testimonial imagery, not the platform itself.
  4. Fourth — mobile-friendliness, tested specifically for large-text and zoom behaviour given the 60–75+ audience, not just a generic mobile-pass check.
  5. Fifth — Service and FAQPage schema on the Sell Your Car hub, the three regional pages and the FAQ page; Review schema deferred until a genuine public review platform exists — never fabricated ahead of that.
  6. HTTPS and security. Standard; confirm the certificate on the new domain or hosting before launch.

4. Content strategy for SEO

Content gap analysis. CarBuyers and We Buy Cars both run mature city-page SEO and, in CarBuyers' case, a blog (competitor-analysis.html); carsales Instant Offer rides the scale of the parent carsales.com.au domain. None of the three visibly publish content for the estate/health-crisis moment or for Northern Rivers specifically (market-gaps.html) — that is the gap to exploit, not out-blogging CarBuyers on generic "how to sell your car" content where it already holds a volume and backlink advantage.

Pillar/cluster model. Pillar: the Sell Your Car hub. Supporting clusters: the three regional pages, Selling a Parent's or Family Member's Car, How It Works, What's Your Car Worth? and FAQ. Every cluster page links back to the pillar and to Testimonials, per website-strategy.html's internal linking direction.

Blog/resource centre. Not started. No confirmed editorial resource or publishing cadence exists yet (audience-teardown.html) — recommendation is to hold off on a formal blog until the FAQ and regional/segment pages are live and measured. If a blog is added later, its natural home is the What's Your Car Worth? content hub, since the weekly radio segment is the one genuinely ongoing content-production source already in place.

Content brief requirements. Every brief must name its target keyword cluster, its search intent, the specific evidence source it draws from (a named Discover or Design document, never an invented specific), and explicitly flag any HIGH-CONTROL-adjacent claim — valuation rules, guarantees, settlement mechanics — that needs Adam's direct confirmation before publishing.

5. On-page optimisation

Title tag formula. Primary local or intent keyword, then a short service descriptor, then the brand name — for example "Sell My Car Northern Rivers | Free Home-Visit Valuation | Adam Hall Buy My Car" (aim for 60 characters or fewer).

Meta description formula. Core promise, geography, a soft call to action — for example "Free, obligation-free home valuation for your car in Lismore, Ballina and across the Northern Rivers. Adam comes to you — no pressure, no strangers, fast payment. Call today." (aim for 155 characters or fewer).

Heading structure. One H1 per page stating its plain-language promise; H2s for each major section matching the section plans in page-strategy.html; H3s only for sub-points within a section — never a skipped level.

Internal linking strategy. Every page links to Testimonials and Contact; regional pages link to each other and to the Sell Your Car hub; link equity flows from the home page directly into the three regional pages and the family-car-sale page, not buried in a footer-only link — those are the pages carrying the open-gap opportunity and need it most.

Image optimisation. Descriptive, specific alt text on every meaningful image — for example "Adam Hall valuing a car in a Currumbin Waters driveway," never a generic "car" or a blank alt attribute; decorative images use an empty alt. Modern compressed formats; explicit width and height to protect layout stability.

URL structure. Lower-case, hyphenated, no dates or IDs — matching sitemap.html's slugs exactly.

6. Local SEO

Applicable, and central — this is fundamentally a local, home-visit service business.

Google Business Profile. Claim or verify the listing; ensure NAP matches the website's single source exactly (never hand-typed differently in more than one place, per the Layer-1 standard); list all three service areas; add the car-buying-service category and real photos of Adam and the home-visit experience, not stock imagery, consistent with brand-strategy.html's imagery direction.

Local citation sources. Standard Australian directories (True Local, Yellow Pages, Yelp AU); distinctively, any citation or mention available through the 4CRB station's own website or sponsor pages — a source no researched competitor can replicate.

Review generation and management. Currently the single largest local-SEO gap — no independent review platform presence exists today (positioning.html proof-points table). Recommendation: a simple, consistent review ask after a well-handled sale, folded into the same conversation as the referral ask already proposed in strategic-priorities.html Bet 3 — one moment, two low-friction asks.

Locally-targeted content. The three regional pages now; town-level content (Lismore, Ballina, Kyogle, Pottsville — all towns existing testimonials already name) only once regional-page traffic is confirmed, not built speculatively ahead of evidence.

7. Link building strategy

Target domain types. Local Australian news and community sites covering the Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers; radio-industry and 4CRB-adjacent pages, a distinctive source no competitor can replicate; local business directories; seniors, aged-care or community-organisation sites relevant to the estate/health-crisis audience, approached carefully and only once messaging for that segment is confirmed with Adam, given the sensitivity involved.

Outreach approach. For local media, pitch the "27 years in the trade, a decade on-air" story angle directly, rather than a generic guest-post pitch. For community and aged-care organisations, a genuine local-service listing request, not a paid placement.

Content-led link building. The What's Your Car Worth? content hub is the strongest linkable asset available — the one piece of content genuinely differentiated from every competitor's generic valuation content, because it is backed by a real, ongoing radio relationship rather than a one-off blog post.

Digital PR angles. The same radio-and-reputation story anchoring brand-strategy.html and positioning.html is the PR angle — a known local identity with 10,000+ on-air valuations is a stronger press hook than any researched competitor can offer.

Toxic link identification / disavow. Not applicable yet — no link profile exists to audit. Revisit once the new site has accumulated inbound links to monitor.

Based on the live competitor research in competitor-analysis.html (20 July 2026). Likely holds rankings on the exact "sell my car [city]" head terms this strategy also targets, making that cluster genuinely contested.

None of the three publish content for the estate/health-crisis moment; at least one (We Buy Cars) and, on this research, the others' localised push both stop short of Northern Rivers NSW; none owns a distinctive, ongoing content asset comparable to a decade-long live radio show — the What's Your Car Worth? hub is structurally unreplicable, matching the same competitive-moat logic already established in positioning.html.

9. AI search & SGE considerations

The adult-child influencer segment increasingly reaches for an AI assistant or generative search rather than a keyword search when researching "how do I sell my father's car" — already named as a live trend in the Discover-phase objectives work. The current five-page brochure site has no structuring for this at all.

Structured data and a plain-text business summary (LocalBusiness, Service and FAQPage JSON-LD, plus an llms.txt file per the Layer-1 standard) are the two highest-leverage, lowest-cost fixes to make the business legible to an AI assistant, not just to Google.

Content format adaptations. FAQ answers written as direct, concise, single-paragraph answers — not buried in marketing copy — are the format most likely to be lifted into an AI overview or cited by an assistant; the FAQ page in page-strategy.html is written with this in mind from the outset, not retrofitted later.

Where AI search may redirect traffic. A generic "sell my car" query is likely to surface carsales- or CarBuyers-scale answers regardless of this site's optimisation. The realistic opportunity is being the cited, named answer for narrower, high-specificity queries — Northern Rivers coverage, the estate/health-crisis situation, "a car buyer that comes to your home" — where the brand's actual differentiation matches the query's specificity.

Mitigation. Concentrate structured-data and content effort on the two open, specific clusters (Northern Rivers, estate/health-crisis) where AI citation is winnable on merit, rather than chasing broad-match AI visibility that would only be won by the scale CarBuyers or carsales already have.

10. Measurement & reporting

Tools required. Google Search Console (indexation and query-level visibility); web analytics (as recommended in website-strategy.html); call tracking that attributes phone conversions to the specific page and keyword that generated them — critical, since a phone call is the primary conversion mechanism here, not a form; a rank-tracking tool for the keyword set in Section 3.

KPIs. Organic sessions by page and cluster; keyword rankings for the mapped terms; click-through rate from Search Console; backlinks acquired; Core Web Vitals scores; and, the metric that actually matters to the business, organic-attributed calls and callback requests, rolling into the same lead-source-split metric already defined in measurement-plan.html.

Reporting cadence. Monthly, folded into the same monthly report measurement-plan.html already establishes for the business's engine-visibility objective — not a separate SEO report living in isolation.

Milestone checkpoints. 3 months — technical foundation live and verified (schema, sitemap, robots, llms.txt), ranking baseline established. 6 months — page-one presence on the open, low-competition clusters (Northern Rivers, estate/health-crisis terms), organic-attributed leads appearing in the monthly report for the first time. 12 months — competitive visibility building on the harder Gold Coast and Brisbane head terms, a public review presence established, and enough data to judge whether SEO is a meaningful lead-source contributor alongside radio and referral — the confirmation, finally, of the channel-mix question the business cannot yet answer.
