Conversion Pathway

How to read this document

This is the designed route to purchase built on customer-journey.html and voiced with the copy in messaging-by-stage.html — how a stranger who has heard of Adam becomes a settled sale. It is written around the two priority segments named in customer-profile.html. Because Adam's 22 CORE questions remain unanswered, several mechanisms below are proposed designs for testing, not confirmed operating procedure — flagged as such throughout. Nothing here describes or invents the payment-to-settlement sequence itself; that remains HIGH-CONTROL, pending Adam's direct confirmation (CQ04), and this document only references that fast, verified payment happens.

The primary pathway

Channel. The 4CRB "What's Your Car Worth?" radio relationship and organic word of mouth/referral remain the two channels with the clearest, if unmeasured, evidence of working (funnel-review.html). The hook at this step is recognition, not a direct offer: a specific, named person the audience has effectively already met.

Capture. Today, capture is entirely the phone call to Adam, with no record kept (sales-process-review.html). Proposed fix, for testing: a simple intake log — caller name, car details, contact preference, source heard from — kept by Adam or support staff at the point of the call, not a digital form replacing the call itself. This is instrumentation behind the human moment, not a substitute for it.

Nurture. There is currently no nurture step at all between first contact and the home visit (funnel-review.html) — most callers move straight to a booked visit or drop out silently. Proposed fix, for testing: a short, warm follow-up (a call or a plainly worded letter, matched to this audience's stated channel preference) for anyone who doesn't book a visit on the first call, giving the "I'll get to it eventually" barrier (buying-triggers-barriers.html) a second, low-pressure chance to convert rather than going quiet forever.

Conversion. The home visit remains the conversion mechanism, exactly as it runs today — Adam values the car in person, the seller decides with genuinely no obligation, and if they agree, paperwork, payment and pick-up follow (sales-process-review.html). This step is not redesigned here; it already works, and the design brief throughout this document is explicit that it must stay human.

Offer and incentive at each conversion point

At capture (first contact). The incentive to act now is the existing offer itself, made explicit rather than assumed: a genuinely free, obligation-free valuation with no cost to finding out. No new incentive is proposed here — the offer already removes the main reason to stall, per audience-teardown.html's "high-conversion readiness" read; adding a discount or deadline-driven device would contradict the "no pressure" brand promise and is not recommended.

At the home visit (purchase decision). The incentive is the same fair, transparently explained number, plus the practical benefits already proven in testimonials — fast payment and free pick-up. Proposed for testing, not yet actioned: a genuinely honest, capacity-based framing (e.g. limited weekly visit slots) once Adam's true hours-per-deal and monthly ceiling are known (pending CQ05, CQ21) — this must never be manufactured scarcity, only an honest reflection of real capacity, per the explicit caution already raised in offer-worksheet.html.

At advocacy. The incentive is social and relational, not financial — being the person who told a friend about a good, safe experience during a hard moment. A financial referral incentive (e.g. a small cash thank-you) is a testable idea worth raising with Adam, but is not assumed here; it risks feeling transactional in exactly the estate/health-driven moments where trust matters most.

Secondary pathway

Not every seller will take the primary radio-or-referral-to-phone-call route — some, particularly Jen's segment researching "how to sell a parent's car" under time pressure, or a younger family member helping an older relative, may reach for a search engine or an AI assistant instead of a phone number they already know. The secondary pathway is a well-answered website and AI-visible presence that gets a cold, digitally-arrived visitor to the same phone call as fast as possible, rather than trying to replicate the trust-building work radio and referral already do. This is worth investing in once the primary pathway's measurement gaps (lead source, conversion rates) are closed enough to show whether digital arrivals are a meaningful share of volume at all, or a smaller, opportunistic edge case — that judgement should not be made on hypothesis.
