PR Brief

The job

Build earned-media presence from zero, distilled from pr-plan.html. This business has no PR history and no existing media relationships beyond the organic 4CRB partnership. Zero press activity runs during Campaign 1 (asset formalisation only, see campaign-calendar.html); once written permission for the 10,000+ valuations claim and testimonials is secured, the working target is 2–4 local or seniors-outlet placements per quarter from month 4 onward.

Audience

Outlets reaching a 60–75+ audience directly: regional daily and community papers across Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers, and dedicated seniors'-interest publications — not general business or motoring trade press. Full tiering below.

The message

Adam as the honest, plain-spoken local voice on selling a car later in life — someone who explains a number rather than imposing one, with 27 years in the trade and a decade proving it live on the radio. Five story angles are ready to pitch: the 27-year trade veteran and the radio milestone; the trust story of selling without a stranger in the room; category-consolidation context (Cars4Us/Toyota, used only as public category news, never implying any relationship or endorsement between the two businesses); service journalism on what to do with a parent's car; and "We come to the Northern Rivers."

Constraints

No outlet named in this brief has an existing relationship or has agreed to anything — every one is a recommended target type to research and approach, not a confirmed placement; confirm current mastheads and submission requirements before any pitch. No journalist should be pitched the "one number, never moved" guarantee or any other high-control valuation/settlement detail until Adam confirms it directly (CQ01–CQ04). No revenue, volume or growth figures from internal discovery material should appear in any public fact sheet without Adam's explicit sign-off. Any consented customer story requires specific, informed consent for media use — a higher bar than website/testimonial consent — and must never be pitched opportunistically around a death, illness or care transition. Adam is the sole spokesperson and sole approver of any public statement; realistic commentary turnaround is 48–72 hours, not same-day.

Success and reporting

No baseline exists for placements or share of voice. A simple manual coverage log (outlet, date, story, link) plus a basic web search or Google Alert is the realistic starting process — no PR monitoring tool is budgeted. Sentiment tagged manually (positive/neutral/negative), reviewed monthly. Any earned backlink is logged and fed to seo-strategy.html. Equivalent advertising value may be reported as a supplementary metric only, never the primary measure. Reported monthly, folded into the same report as every other channel; a quarterly strategic review against these objectives.

First two weeks

  1. Confirm current mastheads, sections and submission requirements for the Tier 1–3 outlets before any pitch — do not treat the list above as a verified, current media kit.
  2. Draft the company boilerplate and spokesperson bio for Adam's review.
  3. Hold all outreach until written permission for the 10,000+ valuations claim and testimonials is secured (Campaign 1 KPI).
  4. Set up the manual coverage log and a Google Alert for the business name.
  5. Draft the crisis-communication holding-statement framework for Adam's sign-off, including who is authorised to respond if Adam is unreachable.
