EDM Brief

The job

Build the email channel from zero, distilled from edm-plan.html. There is no subscriber list, no consented email address on file, and no CRM/EDM system today. This brief builds exactly one working automation — a settlement follow-up and referral ask — and nothing more in year one. Do not build a newsletter, promotional campaigns, or a multi-step nurture sequence; none fit this audience or this business's current scale.

Audience

Two segments only: settled customers (post-sale, advocacy-focused) and callback/enquiry contacts who did not convert (small in number, nurture-focused). The whole list skews 60–75+, phone-first, and prefers plain, unhurried, large-type communication over frequent digital contact (customer-profile.html).

The message

Core promise: a genuine, personal thank-you and a low-pressure referral ask, in Adam's own voice, never a marketing template. Support points: the sale is complete, not "come back and sell again"; one simple way to pass on Adam's number. Voice: warm, personal, unhurried — this is the one written channel where the tone must read exactly like Adam, not like a template (brand-strategy.html).

Assets and inputs supplied

The referral-ask copy already designed in messaging-by-stage.html Stage 6; brand-guidelines.html type and colour direction for a large-type, accessible template; crm-brief.html's two sync fields (settlement date, consent status/date) for whichever CRM this list ends up connected to.

Constraints

No purchased list, scraped contact or existing contact import — none exists to import from, and none would carry consent under the Australian Spam Act. Double opt-in is not recommended for this list size; single opt-in with a clear unsubscribe is the standard, pending legal-compliance-advisor confirmation of the exact consent wording. No A/B testing in year one — realistic list size is too small to reach significance. No newsletter, no promotional/discount email — this business's offer is a fair valuation, not a discount mechanic. Data handling (retention period, storage security, disclosure wording) is flagged for direct legal-compliance-advisor review, not invented in this brief — the same flag is carried in crm-brief.html and paid-media-brief.html.

Success and reporting

No month-1 subscriber target is meaningful — there is no baseline. First milestone: the list-building mechanism itself live by month 6–9. The settlement follow-up should cover 100% of settled sales with consent on file by month 6–9. Revenue attribution is not the right frame — email's realistic contribution is referral and repeat advocacy, not direct transactional revenue. Reported monthly (sends, opens, unsubscribes), folded into the same report as every other channel — no separate dashboard.

First two weeks

  1. Draft the plain-language consent line for the settlement conversation and the callback-form opt-in, for legal-compliance-advisor review before use.
  2. Select an ESP against the must-have criteria above and confirm SPF/DKIM/DMARC on the sending domain.
  3. Build the single settlement-follow-up template — large type, single column, one call to action.
  4. Draft the one-email settlement-follow-up copy in Adam's voice, drawing on messaging-by-stage.html Stage 6.
  5. Agree with Adam how consent will be recorded at settlement until a CRM exists to hold it.
