## Purpose

This document sets the Design phase strategy for Adam Hall Buy My Car. It is a pre-validation draft – nothing here is confirmed with Adam yet, but the shape we intend to test with him.

Design has one job: turn what lives in Adam's head into things other people can run. The asset we are building is the Adam Hall Operating System – the documented version of how the business actually works – plus the brand and sales architecture around it. Every artefact should make Adam's judgement usable by someone who is not Adam.

## What Design Inherits from Discover

Design does not invent. It consumes the Re-Discover mapping output – sessions capturing Adam's decision rules, trust technique and operating patterns in LIGHT and FULL mode – and turns that into SOPs, scripts, valuation rules, hiring briefs, brand direction and training content.

The structure below is a target, not a finished set. Some cells stay thin until a matching session happens. A visible gap beats an invented answer.

## Customer Profile (Hypothesis)

**PAIN.** Mostly 60–75+, selling a car tied to a life change – downsizing, losing a licence, an estate after a death, a health event that ends driving. The car can be a late parent's, or the last big decision before a smaller life. Fear of being ripped off runs high, and so does fear of the process – paperwork, technology, strangers.

**GAIN.** Relief, not a negotiation win. A fair number delivered without pressure, plain-language explanation at each step, and certainty the money and paperwork will be handled properly.

**PROOF.** Word of mouth and Adam's radio heritage are believed to be the dominant proof mechanism today – hypothesis, to be confirmed. Home visits appear to be a key proof moment for anxious sellers. Volume and repeat pattern (~25 cars/month, ~90% retail) suggest trust is earned and retained, but the proof points customers actually cite have not yet been mapped.

## Brand Strategy Direction (Hypothesis)

Positioning direction: trust before transaction. Adam Hall Buy My Car is not competing on the cheapest offer – it is competing on being the offer the customer does not need to second-guess. The Adam Hall Standard is the operating proof: empathy first then the solution, ownership not excuses, staying in the room until the customer is genuinely settled. That should carry into every channel, not just tone of voice.

**Open question – flagged, not decided:** does the brand carry Adam's personal name? His name is the current trust asset, built through decades of radio presence and word of mouth. But a name-carrying brand is harder to sell and scale past one person, which cuts against a brand that does not depend on him. This is the biggest brand decision in the engagement, and it needs Adam's answer.

## Offer Architecture (Hypothesis)

Core offer, as currently understood: a fast, fair, no-pressure valuation and purchase, handled with the same care whether the sale nets $500 or $50,000 gross. The ~90/10 retail/wholesale split suggests most cars move through a channel that protects margin – the ~$4,200 average retail gross per car is the number to keep protecting as volume scales toward $7M. Speed and certainty for the customer, margin discipline for the business. Needs testing against real valuation examples before it is locked.

Three assets touch money, identity or ownership transfer and are marked high-control. Nothing in those rows is treated as documented until Adam confirms it directly.

## Sales on a Page (Draft)

| Stage | What happens | Owner | Trust moment |

| Qualification | Adam sorts genuine sellers, gathers car and situation detail | Adam | Listening for the real reason behind the sale |

Draft shape only. Stage ownership and any handoffs beyond Adam need testing against real cases.

## Team and Hiring Design

The Standard has to be hired for, not trained in after the fact – George is the proof of what happens when skills are hired first and values are assumed. The hiring brief sets the filter: empathy first then the solution, daily self-criticism, ownership not excuses, staying in the room. Skills are teachable against the SOPs once those values are in place; the values are not.

A first hire's first 90 days should prove the person can hold the Standard under real pressure, not just follow a script. What that proof looks like is an open question, below.

## Gate 2 Criteria

Design closes when:

- Customer Profile, Brand Strategy Direction and Offer Architecture are tested with Adam, moved from hypothesis to validated, or flagged as open.
- The naming question (CQ11) has an answer, even if it is "not yet."
- The Operating System inventory has real content behind every non-high-control row.
- All three high-control assets are confirmed directly with Adam, with no invented compliance detail.
- The hiring brief and onboarding programme are specific enough to run a real hire through.
- Adam has signed off the Sales on a Page draft as accurate.
