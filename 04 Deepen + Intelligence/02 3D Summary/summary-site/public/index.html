The method behind the work03 / 10

The 3D Process.

Three disciplined phases, three decision gates. The 3D Process is the Jewell Projects method for building a brand and its go-to-market on evidence rather than opinion. Each phase ends at a gate, a point to review, align and approve before the next one starts. The work compounds in one direction instead of looping.

· 3D Process
01
Checkpoint 1 · Gate 1

Discover

Find the opportunity. Audience teardown, competitor analysis, the offer, and the market truth, reviewed at Gate 1.

02
Checkpoint 2 · Gate 2

Design

Design the system. Brand, customer, business and marketing strategy, messaging and the website, locked at Gate 2.

03
Checkpoint 3 · Gate 3

Deploy

Take it to market. The launch playbook, channels, CRM and measurement, approved at Gate 3.

Then Deepen: what is deployed feeds back into discovery, an ongoing loop that compounds.

The whole engagement on one page04 / 10

The 3D Process, on a page.

Sixty-two documents is the right depth to build from. It is not what gets read on a Tuesday. So here is the whole engagement in six lines, the belief, the position, the numbers, the constraint, the plan, the decision.

The belief
A trust-conversion business wearing a car-buying shell. The product being sold is Adam, not the car.
The position
The only named, personally accountable buyer for sellers 60 to 75+ across the Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers. Every competitor is a company, a branch or a panel.
The numbers
About $5M a year, around 25 cars a month, roughly $4,200 average retail gross. More than 10,000 live on-air valuations on 4CRB since 2010, and zero paid marketing. All to be validated with you.
The constraint
Demand and trust already work. The business lives in one person's head, and nothing is measured.
The plan
Build capacity before buying demand. Run it inside, then put the name up, then scale the buying.
The decision
Close the 22 CORE questions. The answers promote the set from hypothesis to fact.

CORE — Customers, Offering, Rivals, Expression — is the shape of the business. The 22 killer questions hang off it: ten for Discover, six for Design, six for Deploy. Every one is still open.
