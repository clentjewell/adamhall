01 Overview

A conversation, not a car yard

The whole identity springs from one idea: buying a car from Adam should feel like a friendly chat, not a negotiation. The logo is a speech bubble; the voice is warm and plain-spoken; the promise is a hassle-free guarantee. Everything below keeps that conversational, trustworthy tone — grounded green, a soft lilac lift, and a wordmark that literally reads “Buy my Car.”

The promiseHassle-free guarantee
Tagline. “Hassle-free car buying, let’s chat…” — the invitation that sits under the mark and sets the tone for every touchpoint.

02 Primary logo

The primary logo

The signature lockup: the “Adam Hall” name above a bold BUY my CAR wordmark, set inside a rounded speech bubble with its dropped dot “tail”. This is the default mark — reach for it first, and give it room to breathe.

Primary — on lightPaper / white grounds
Reversed — on darkOutline / knockout
Clear space & tail. Keep clear space around the bubble equal to the height of the “Adam Hall” line, and never separate the dropped dot from the bubble — the tail is part of the mark.

04 Monochrome

Monochrome logo

For single-colour print, embossing, stamps and low-ink situations, the mark reduces to a clean outline (reversed) or a solid one-colour fill. No gradients, no effects — just the shape.

12 Typography

Typography

The identity pairs a bold geometric sans for the wordmark and headlines with a casual brush script for accents (the my, “hello”, “welcome”) and Adam’s handwritten signature for the personal touch. The specimen below sets headings and UI in the web-safe equivalents used across the applications.

Display / Headline Let’s chat about your car
Section head Hassle-free, start to finish
Body Adam comes to you, values your car, and makes a fair offer — no lots, no pressure, no games. A simple conversation from first hello to handshake.
UI / Buttons CHAT WITH ADAM  ·  HOW IT WORKS  ·  0400 000 000
Script accent
Rule of thumb. One script moment per layout. The brush script and signature are seasoning — used sparingly they feel personal; overused they read as busy.

13 Imagery style

Imagery style

Real, warm, human. Photography shows genuine people — especially older sellers at their own homes — in candid, unhurried moments. People first, the car second; a relationship, not a transaction.

14 In practice

The identity in practice

How the pieces come together across the touchpoints that matter — the website, the email sign-off, and the card that changes hands at the door.

Website hero

Green ground, reversed logo, lilac framing and a photo of Adam — with the phone number and “Chat with Adam” as the primary calls to action.

Email signature

The logo, name and role, phone and web — a compact, consistent sign-off that carries the brand into every reply.

Business card

A lilac face that opens the conversation (“hello · welcome · your car’s value”) and a green reverse with Adam’s details — the mark leading both.
