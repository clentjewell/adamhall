## Marketplace site demo

- Ronnie rebuilt the marketplace on a new platform optimised for SEO and GEO (AI-bot traffic), with animated car photos on listing cards.
- The cars-for-sale page shows ~25 cars a month, with sold cars staying visible for 30 days as social proof — buyers follow Adam's listings and refer friends.
- Each listing includes full specs, a trust block with Adam's personal note, PPSR status, service history, and a finance repayment estimate.
- A compare tool puts two cars side by side across odometer, price, fuel, drivetrain, weekly repayment and more.

## Dealer console and admin tools

- Password-gated dealer console: dashboard (live listings, new submissions, sales this month), submissions/CRM, inventory management, finance enquiries, site copy editor, analytics, and a site guide.
- Inventory changes in the admin reflect live on the public marketplace immediately.
- Listing statuses: available, reserved (deposit paid / finance pending), and sold — Adam can take backup enquiries on reserved cars.
- Analytics tracks sold cars, margin by make, offer acceptance rate, and average time to offer.

## Adam AI and listing automation

- Adam AI is an in-platform assistant trained on Adam's voice, tone and business rules — any team member can use it for listing copy, social posts, buyer replies and sourcing strategy.
- Live demo generated a listing for a 2017 Subaru Impreza 2.0i-L with 35,000 km. Adam flagged two style rules to embed: always "factory alloy wheels" (not "alloys"), and always "automatic transmission" (not "CVT" — buyers are put off by CVT).
- Adam's current process: ~5 minutes per listing typed manually to George, who then spends ~30 minutes adding photos and copy-pasting to Facebook Marketplace. Goal: Adam gets to 90% done via voice input, with Liz tweaking and pushing live.
- Differentiation: dealerships using generic AI get brochure-style copy; here Adam's taste and judgment are layered over the model, making every description personal.

## Instant offer / car-buying tool

- Adam wants a "value my car" submission tool similar to Cars4Us and Carsales Instant Offer — seller enters car details and gets a response.
- The tool will not auto-generate a price; it captures seller details and routes the enquiry to Adam to assess and respond. Ronnie will build this into the submissions flow.
- Adam's reasoning: he pre-qualifies before committing, and for cars he won't buy (e.g. high-risk brands like Land Rover) he declines politely and suggests alternatives, keeping the relationship intact.
- Ronnie will also build an optional internal instant-offer view so Adam can generate a ballpark figure for himself when responding.

## House sale marketplace as proof of concept

- Clent showed a moving-sale storefront built for his own house (10 Buncrana Terrace) on the same platform — Liz ran the back end, managed enquiries and set pickup windows (Saturdays 9–11am).
- The house sale liquidated in a week with ~$5,000 from the fridge alone, without Clent answering calls or negotiating directly.

## Facebook Marketplace integration

- There is no automatic API to push listings from the admin to Facebook Marketplace — it requires manual copy-paste of description and photos.
- Adam currently lists under George's Facebook account to keep separation between his personal profile and the buy/sell activity — he doesn't want sellers seeing a car they sold him relisted much higher two days later.
- Plan: use Adam's personal Facebook account (required to run a business page) but keep public-facing listings under the Buy My Car brand, not his personal profile.
