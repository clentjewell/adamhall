## What you can already click

**The buyer side.** Every car has its own page with a photo gallery, a PPSR badge, its inspection and service history, and your take written in plain words. There is a slot for a walk-around video, and buttons to enquire or book a look. Buyers filter the inventory by what they want, and sold cars stay visible for thirty days before they archive themselves.

**The seller side.** A four-step form takes a seller from rego to condition to photos to contact, and it saves as they go so nobody loses their place. Once they submit, they get a status page they can check any time with no account and no login. From any car page a seller can start a trade-in in the same flow.

**The admin back office.** This is where your judgement lives in software. Submissions move through a pipeline from New to Reviewing to Offer made to Accepted, Declined or Settled. A valuation worksheet holds your offer, the expected retail and the recon, and works the margin out for you. You send a templated offer or a kind decline in one click. There is an enquiries inbox, an inventory manager, and a settlement checklist that covers PPSR, the payout letter, ID, rego transfer and funds, with every tick stamped with who did it and when. Nothing about money or paperwork happens without you.

## Online channels: Google Business Profile and the proof engine

Here is the honest part. You have no independent public reviews today. That is the single biggest gap between you and a stranger deciding to trust you, and it is the first thing we fix.

We claim and verify your Google Business Profile, set one name, one address and one phone number everywhere they appear, list all three service areas, put you in the car-buying-service category, and add real photos. Then we build the review engine: a systematic, scripted ask that goes to customers after settlement, so public proof starts to accumulate from zero. Rex in Kyogle already says he will only sell his cars to you. The job is to capture that and multiply it.

## Social, ongoing: Facebook and Facebook Marketplace

Your audience is on Facebook, so Facebook is the platform. We start at two posts a week and build to three or four, across five content pillars led by A Person, Not a Company and Relief, Not Another Task.

Facebook Marketplace is new in this proposal, and it fits your buyers well. We syndicate every car in stock to Facebook Marketplace, with each listing linking back to your website. Your cars show up where local people already scroll, and every conversation lands back on your own site where you control it.

4CRB stays your anchor, unchanged. A decade on air and more than ten thousand live valuations since 2010 is a reach most dealers would pay anything for, and it keeps doing its job. We systemise the referral ask around all of it: scripted, tracked, and asked every time rather than when you remember.

## Search and AI search: now and next

You asked in July for the site to be visible to AI chatbots and generative search, not only Google. That splits cleanly into now and next.

**Now.** Most of the technical foundation ships with the build already: every car page carries Vehicle structured data, per-car social previews and a sitemap. We add LocalBusiness and FAQ structured data, connect Google Search Console, publish an llms.txt file, stand up the Google Business Profile, and set a baseline so any movement is measurable. GEO means getting quoted when someone asks an assistant how to sell their dad's car near Tweed Heads. That comes from structured data plus plain FAQ answers written so an assistant can lift them whole.

**Next.** Once the foundation is settling, we write content on the two clusters where nobody competes: the Northern Rivers, and estate or health-driven sales such as selling a parent's car. We add review structured data once real reviews exist. Town-level pages for Lismore, Ballina, Kyogle and Pottsville wait until regional traffic proves they are worth building.

## Email

Email starts from zero and stays consent-only. We only ever email settled customers and people who enquired through the site. We never buy a list.

The first automation is the one that earns its keep straight away: a single settlement follow-up and referral ask, sent three to five days after a car settles, written in your voice. On the buyer side, the site can already alert someone when a car they want comes in, and the enquiry, offer and decline emails already send from the admin. A newsletter and longer sequences wait until the list is real enough to be worth the effort.

## Paid ads: when and how

We keep the July gate on this, because it protects your money. No spend until one full month of lead-log baseline exists and call tracking is live. The phone call is the conversion in your business, so tracking calls is not optional.

Once that is in place, we run two channels. Facebook to two audiences only: downsizers 55 and up, and adult children 35 to 55 handling a parent's car, both geo-fenced to your areas. Google on search only, on high-intent terms like "sell my car gold coast" and the low-competition "sell my father's car" cluster. Manual bidding the first month so we see what is really happening before anything scales. We size the budget once you confirm your spend history (CQ18).
