Discover Summary

What this is

This is the CP1 phase gate for Adam Hall Buy My Car — a one-page distillation of all fourteen Discover documents already produced, for review and sign-off in a client meeting. Full detail sits in the documents named throughout; nothing here is new research.

Ten things we now know

  • This is a trust-conversion business wearing a car-buying shell — the product being sold is Adam, not the car (intelligence-brief-digest.html).
  • The constraint is not demand or trust — both already work, at ~25 cars/month on trust alone with no paid marketing spend. The constraint is that everything lives in one person's head (intelligence-brief-digest.html).
  • The audience is 60–75+, trust-first and price-second, split across three segments: downsizers, licence-loss sellers, and estate/health-driven family sellers (audience-teardown.html, customer-segments.html).
  • Every named direct competitor — CarBuyers, We Buy Cars, carsales Instant Offer — is a company or platform with rotating staff or a dealer panel; none puts one known, accountable person at the centre of the sale (competitor-analysis.html).
  • The sharpest, most repeated complaint against price-led competitors is that the number moves after inspection — because a “one number, never moved” promise is open ground (category-positioning.html, differentiation-opportunities.html).
  • No researched competitor markets to the estate or health-crisis seller — this is Adam's segment to own outright (market-gaps.html).
  • Nothing about the business is measured — no lead volume, no conversion rate, no Adam-hours per deal. Measurement itself, not demand, is the weakest link (funnel-review.html).
  • The ~$4,200 average retail gross per car across a ~90% retail/10% wholesale split is the only reliable unit-economics number that exists today (offer-worksheet.html, pricing-packaging-notes.html).
  • None of Adam's 22 CORE questions have been answered yet — every audience, brand and offer hypothesis in this Discover phase is unvalidated until he answers them directly (core-questions-digest.html).
  • Growth from $5M to $7M and beyond depends on removing the one-person ceiling before adding demand — more leads into the current model does not equal more sellable revenue (intelligence-brief-digest.html, funnel-review.html).

Open questions

  • Does the brand carry Adam's personal name, or a name sellable independently of him? (CQ11)
  • Where do leads actually come from today, and what share from each source? (CQ06)
  • What does “sellable” look like in numbers, in five years? (CQ08)
  • What is the true hours-per-deal, and at what monthly number does settlement start to break? (CQ05, CQ21)
  • How does a referral happen today — is it asked for, or does it just occur? (CQ07, CQ16)

Audience & Market

  • Downsizers — retirees reducing to one car; calm, largest, most reachable segment.
  • Licence-loss sellers — forced by a medical/licensing deadline; small but high-intent the moment they appear.
  • Estate/health-driven sellers — a family member selling on someone else's behalf; emotionally heaviest, highest advocacy value.
  • Niche: a trust-first, 60–75+ car seller who wants relief and a fair number, not the last dollar of a private sale.
  • Primary persona: Barbara, 71, the downsizer — full detail in audience-teardown.html.

Category & Competitors

  • CarBuyers — “best price guarantee”; 3.2/5 on ProductReview with a recurring post-inspection price-drop complaint.
  • We Buy Cars — branch-appointment model, SE Queensland only, no Northern Rivers coverage.
  • carsales Instant Offer — national platform scale, impersonal dealer-panel handoff, showed limited availability at time of research.
  • Primary differentiators: named-person continuity, and a decade-long radio-and-referral proof engine (10,000+ on-air valuations) no competitor can replicate.
  • Competitive angle: “the name you already trust, at your kitchen table” — trust-and-ease-maximising, fully human, an empty position in this market.

Offer & Commercial Diagnosis

  • Core offer: a free, obligation-free home-visit valuation, followed by a direct purchase, fast (generally 24-hour) payment and free pick-up.
  • Primary transformation: from the dreaded private-sale slog to one relieved phone call and a fair number.
  • Price range/structure: no published price; ~$4,200 average retail gross per car; ~90% retail / 10% wholesale split.
  • Strongest proof point: 10,000+ live, on-air valuations since 2010 on 4CRB's “What's Your Car Worth?”, plus five named customer testimonials.
