Jobs To Be Done

How to read this document

These are the jobs the segments in customer-segments.html hire Adam Hall Buy My Car for, built on the pains in customer-pain-points.html and the triggers in buying-triggers-barriers.html. None of these job statements have been tested with Adam or with customers directly — they are a structured hypothesis drawn from the confirmed customer profile and site content, offered to sharpen offer and messaging decisions, not as a validated model.

Job statements

Job 1 — The trusted handover

"When I need to sell a car I've owned for years, I want to hand the whole process to someone I trust, so I can be free of it without worrying I've been taken advantage of."

Current hire: nobody, or a reluctant private-sale attempt via marketplace listings. Firing criteria: any sense of being pressured, rushed, or given a number that feels arbitrary rather than explained. Success criteria: a fair number, explained plainly, delivered without pressure, and a clean, fast finish.

Job 2 — The fast, dignified exit

"When my licence is gone and I need to sell quickly, I want a fast and respectful sale, so I can move on without the situation being drawn out or exposed."

Current hire: a rushed private sale or a dealership trade-in accepted mainly for speed. Firing criteria: any process that takes longer than expected, or that requires the seller to keep re-explaining their situation. Success criteria: sold within days, for a fair price, with minimal back-and-forth.

Job 3 — Taking one thing off the list

"When I'm managing a parent's affairs after a death or health decline, I want the car handled properly by someone else, so I can focus on my family instead of a transaction."

Current hire: the family member themselves, doing it reluctantly alongside everything else, or leaving the car unsold for longer than necessary. Firing criteria: any interaction that feels transactional or insensitive to the situation. Success criteria: one visit, one call, respectfully handled, genuinely off the list.

Job 4 — Proof the number is fair

"When I don't know what my car is really worth, I want a credible, independent-feeling valuation, so I can be confident I'm not being short-changed."

Current hire: online valuation tools, asking around, or simply accepting whatever a dealership offers. Firing criteria: a number that seems to change, or that isn't explained in terms the seller understands. Success criteria: a number that matches or beats their private expectation, delivered with a clear explanation of how it was reached.

Job 5 — Avoiding strangers and technology

"When I have to sell a car, I want to avoid dealing with unknown buyers and unfamiliar apps, so I don't feel exposed or out of my depth."

Current hire: reluctant use of Facebook Marketplace or Gumtree, sometimes with a family member's help to manage the listing. Firing criteria: any requirement to use an app, take extensive photos, or negotiate directly with an unknown buyer. Success criteria: the whole process handled by one known person, largely by phone and in person, with no technology burden on the seller.

Job 6 — Certainty the paperwork is right

"When I sell my car, I want to be sure the paperwork, payment and ownership transfer are done properly, so I'm not exposed to a problem later."

Current hire: uncertainty and hope, when selling privately without expertise in transfer or payment risk. Firing criteria: any ambiguity about who is responsible for what, or delay in payment after the car has been handed over. Success criteria: same-day or next-day payment, clear guidance through the paperwork, and confidence it has been done correctly. Opportunity call

Own Job 3 (taking one thing off the list) and Job 6 (certainty the paperwork is right) deliberately. Job 3 is currently served well in practice (the testimonials prove it) but not messaged or targeted as its own job — most of the site's language speaks to the transaction, not to the relief of delegation during a hard season of life. Naming this job explicitly in messaging (rather than leaving it implicit in testimonials) would sharpen appeal to the highest-advocacy segment. Job 6 matters because it is the job most likely to be underestimated by an older, less digitally confident seller until it goes wrong somewhere else — being explicit and reassuring about payment and paperwork certainty, without overstating anything ahead of Adam confirming the actual settlement sequence (CQ04), is a low-risk, high-trust message to own now.
