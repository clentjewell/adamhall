Success Definition

How to read this document

This is the closing distillation of the Discover phase — what success means for Adam Hall Buy My Car, made as precise as the current evidence allows. It draws on all fourteen Discover documents already produced (audience-teardown.html and its Audience & Market siblings; competitor-analysis.html and its Category & Competitors siblings; offer-worksheet.html and its Offer & Commercial Diagnosis siblings) plus USER.html and the digests in memory/digests/. No client-facing yes-criteria digest (a “bant.html”) exists for this engagement: qualification here runs through Jewell Projects’ own parallel 3D Process, not a Maxxim BANT digest, so this document uses USER.html and intelligence-brief-digest.html as the equivalent source of the client’s own stated success frame. That gap is flagged plainly rather than papered over.

In their own words

Adam Hall has not yet answered any of the 22 CORE questions (CQ01–CQ22) that anchor the parallel Jewell Projects engagement — see core-questions-digest.html. No verbatim success statement from Adam exists yet in the source material. The closest available proxy is the strategic thesis Jewell Projects has already agreed with Adam as the frame for the whole engagement: “freedom and a sale are the same build” — the idea that documenting Adam’s judgement into a transferable system is the one move that both frees his time and makes the business saleable (source: USER.html, intelligence-brief-digest.html). Alongside this sit three confirmed growth figures: current revenue of approximately $5M, a near-term target of $7M, and a longer “10x path” benchmarked against Cars4Us — a Gold Coast/Brisbane car-buying business that grew from a 2019 founding to a reported $120M sale to Toyota Tsusho in 2026 (source: intelligence-brief-digest.html; competitor-analysis.html).

Flagged: these are the confirmed engagement-level figures Jewell Projects has already captured from Adam, not a verbatim, in-session success statement. The live CORE mapping sessions are the mechanism to capture Adam’s own definition of success directly — see recommended-next-moves.html for how this feeds the Design-phase sequence.

Made measurable

  • Revenue. Grow from ~$5M to $7M annual revenue by a target date TBD — propose 18 months from Design-phase kick-off (confirm with Adam), without increasing reliance on Adam personally attending every visit.
  • Capacity documented. A second buyer trained and operating to the Adam Hall Standard, handling a defined share of visits unsupervised, within 12 months of Design-phase sign-off — date TBD, and directly answering CQ20 (who the second buyer is) and CQ22 (the number that proves the business runs without Adam).
  • Measurement in place. Lead volume, lead source split, lead-to-visit and visit-to-sale conversion rates, and Adam-hours per deal tracked monthly from the first full month after Discover sign-off — the precondition for every key result in objectives-key-results.html (see funnel-review.html, which confirms none of this is tracked today).
  • Referral systemised. A tested, repeatable referral ask in Adam’s own words (CQ16), live and tracked, generating a measurable, named share of monthly leads within 90 days of Design-phase sign-off — date TBD.
  • Sale-ready milestone. A numeric definition of “sellable” (CQ08) confirmed directly with Adam and adopted as the north-star key result for the multi-year 10x path, benchmarked against Cars4Us’ roughly seven-year build-to-sale timeline (2019–2026).

Explicitly not the goal

  • Chasing lead volume or paid media spend before capacity and measurement exist — more leads into an unmeasured, one-person system adds strain, not growth (funnel-review.html).
  • Competing on headline price against national or multi-branch buyers such as CarBuyers, We Buy Cars or carsales Instant Offer (differentiation-opportunities.html) — this engagement does not chase the cheapest-offer customer.
  • Vanity metrics — social followers, website traffic or brand-awareness scores with no traceable link to a booked home visit or a completed sale.
  • Automating or replacing the trust moment itself — the home visit, the reassurance of a grieving or anxious seller. Per USER.html, these are documented for training only, never automated away.
  • Any public claim about valuation logic, settlement or payment mechanics ahead of Adam’s direct confirmation. HIGH-CONTROL discipline applies throughout (USER.html, core-questions-digest.html).
  • Pre-empting the brand-naming decision (CQ11 — whether the brand carries Adam’s personal name) in pursuit of a faster marketing win.

Early signals

Within the first weeks, before any headline revenue number can move, these signals show whether the engagement is on or off track:

  • CORE mapping sessions underway on schedule (two a week while Clent Jewell is in-country, per phase1-work-digest.html), with CQ01–CQ10 answered and confirmed in writing.
  • A basic lead log or call register in use for every enquiry, even informally — the first fix flagged in sales-process-review.html.
  • Every incoming enquiry tagged with its source (radio, word of mouth, website, other) from day one, closing the gap flagged in funnel-review.html.
  • A draft referral-ask script tested, in Adam’s own words, on the next few completed sales.
  • No HIGH-CONTROL claim (valuation logic, settlement sequence, payment mechanics) has been published or promised publicly ahead of Adam’s direct confirmation.
