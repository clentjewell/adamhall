Offer Worksheet

General Info

Adam Hall Buy My Car is a home-visit, direct car-buying service covering the Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers. The business model is an owner-operated direct-purchase and resale trade: Adam personally values a customer’s car at their home, buys it outright if the seller agrees, then moves the car on through one of two channels — retail (roughly 90% of volume, carrying the business’s margin) or wholesale (roughly 10%, for cars that don’t suit retail). It is not a marketplace, a consultancy or a subscription service; it is a trade-purchase business built entirely around one person’s judgement and reputation. A separate e-commerce marketplace website is scoped as future work but does not exist yet — see product-service-review.html.

Target market: car owners, predominantly aged 60–75 and older, across the Gold Coast, Brisbane and Northern Rivers, who are selling a car because of a life change — downsizing, losing a driving licence, or a family member selling on behalf of someone who has died or can no longer drive. Current State & Goals

From the seller’s side: an older car owner facing a car they no longer want or need is often afraid of two different things at once — being ripped off, and the process itself. Private sale means cleaning the car, fielding messages from strangers, negotiating, and hoping the buyer actually turns up with the money. Trading in at a dealership means a number handed down with no real explanation. Neither feels safe, and for a family member selling a parent’s or late relative’s car, the stakes feel higher still.

What this customer wants is not the highest possible dollar figure — it’s relief: a fair number, explained plainly, delivered by someone who won’t pressure them, with the money and the paperwork handled properly and quickly.

Solution Overview

Adam Hall comes to the seller’s home, gives a free, obligation-free, expert valuation on the spot, and — if the seller wants to proceed — buys the car directly, handles the simple paperwork there and then, pays by fast bank transfer generally within 24 hours, and arranges free pick-up, so the seller never has to advertise, negotiate, or meet a stranger alone.

Benefits

  • No advertising, cleaning, staging or fielding calls from strangers — the whole private-sale workload disappears.
  • A same-day or next-day payment once a price is agreed, generally within 24 hours, paid by OSKO bank transfer.
  • One named, known person handles the visit, the valuation, the paperwork and the pick-up — not a call centre or a rotating sales team.
  • A free, obligation-free valuation with no pressure to accept — the seller can say no with nothing lost.
  • Free car pick-up once a sale is agreed, at a time that suits the seller.
  • Cars unsuitable for the service are told so upfront, rather than wasting the seller’s time. [Feature confirmed on site; whether this is applied consistently is not independently verified.]

Offer Structure

Today there is effectively one offer: a free home-visit valuation, followed — if the seller agrees — by an immediate purchase, with payment generally within 24 hours and free pick-up. There is no tiering, no published package levels and no formal service-level commitment beyond what the website states.

[Hypothesis, flagged for testing with Adam] A three-part offer architecture has been proposed internally but not yet confirmed: an entry tier (the free home-visit valuation, removing the seller’s first fear), a commercial spine (the retail purchase itself, carrying the ~$4,200 average retail gross), and an LTV engine (referrals and repeat family transactions). This is a shape to test, not a locked structure — see pricing-packaging-notes.html for the packaging detail.

Guarantees

The functional guarantee today is the obligation-free valuation itself: the seller can walk away with no cost and no commitment. There is no stated satisfaction guarantee, refund policy or formal service-level agreement beyond the “generally 24 hours” payment commitment. Recommendation: consider formalising a plain-language promise (e.g. a payment-timing commitment, or a right to decline any offer with zero cost) as a named guarantee in future marketing — this is a suggestion for the partner to consider, not a claim already made.

Urgency & Scarcity

No urgency or scarcity mechanism exists in the current offer, and none is manufactured on the website. The real constraint is capacity: Adam personally attends every visit and makes every decision, so the business is capped at roughly 25 cars a month regardless of demand — this is an operational limit, not a marketing lever, and should not be advertised as scarcity until the operating system is documented and a second buyer exists (see sales-process-review.html). [Recommendation, not yet actioned]: once capacity is better understood, a genuine, honestly-framed limited-appointments message (e.g. weekly visit slots) could be tested as a campaign device.

Consequences of Inaction

If a seller doesn’t act, the car keeps depreciating, keeps costing money to insure and register, and keeps sitting as an unresolved task — often an emotionally loaded one for a family managing a parent’s estate or a health decline. Left to a private sale, the seller risks scam buyers, no-shows, lowball haggling and the physical burden of preparing and advertising the car. Left to a dealership trade-in, they risk an unexplained, likely lower number with no real conversation about why.
