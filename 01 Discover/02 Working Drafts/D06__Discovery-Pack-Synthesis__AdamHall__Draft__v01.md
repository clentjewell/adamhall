The constraint is that the conversion system, the mechanism that turns a nervous 68-year-old into a signed, settled sale, exists nowhere but Adam's head – invisible to a hire, unhireable as a role, unsellable as a business.

## What We Know (Confirmed)

Adam Hall Buy My Car buys 20–30 cars a month (around 25) at roughly $4,200 retail gross per car, 90% retail and 10% wholesale. Revenue is approximately $5M, targeting $7M and a longer-run 10x. The customer base is predominantly 60–75 and older: downsizers, licence-loss sellers, and estate or health-driven sellers, for whom trust matters more than price. Adam carries the valuation logic, customer-trust technique, yes/no decision and payment and paperwork discipline, none of it documented. The Adam Hall Standard – empathy first then the solution, daily self-criticism, ownership not excuses, stay in the room, value your time commercially – is the cultural spine; the business has seen the cost of hiring skill before values, in George. Adam's radio presence gives the brand a public voice most competitors lack. Engagement approved 14 July 2026: Re-Discover, then Design, then Activate through Maxxim, with JewellAI keeping the system current after. The same method has already run for BTC.

## Business and Model

This is a trust-conversion business operating inside a car-buying shell, not a car business that happens to be trusted. At ~25 cars a month and $4,200 retail gross, unit economics are healthy, but every transaction depends on Adam personally reading the seller, setting the number and calling the yes or no. The 90/10 split implies a discipline about what is worth retailing, a rule that also lives only in his judgement. Growth from $5M to $7M under the current model means more of Adam – the opposite of what a 10x, sellable brand needs.

## Audience Synthesis (Hypothesis)

The baseline points to three segments inside the 60–75+ base: downsizers, licence-loss sellers needing to sell fast and with dignity, and estate or health-driven sellers, often family acting on someone else's behalf. All three are price-second, trust-first, and reward a calm, unhurried process. This is a hypothesis built from the March baseline and Adam's general description of customers, not yet tested segment by segment for volume, margin or referral behaviour. Confirm in the live sessions, not by assumption.

## Brand Direction (Hypothesis)

Adam's radio heritage gives this brand something most car-buying operators lack: existing public recognition attached to a name. The hypothesis to test in Design is whether that recognition can be separated from Adam-the-person and rebuilt as Adam Hall Buy My Car-the-brand, so the trust attached to him personally becomes attached to a system a buyer could acquire. Unconfirmed, and central to the growth thesis.

## Market and White Space

Everyone buys cars. Nobody makes selling one feel safe.

## Offer Synthesis (Hypothesis)

Proposed for testing: three tiers with a named spine. Entry tier – a free home-visit valuation, removing the first fear point for an older seller who does not want to drive to a yard or deal with strangers online. Commercial spine – the retail buy itself, carrying the $4,200 gross. LTV engine – referrals and repeat family transactions, where adult children, neighbours and estate contacts become the next lead. The pathway between tiers is not yet mapped and needs Adam's own account of how it happens today.

## Bottlenecks and Risks

The key-person ceiling caps volume at roughly 25 cars a month because every decision routes through one calendar. Undocumented valuation logic means delegating buying decisions carries real margin risk until Adam's rules are written down and tested. Trust is held personally, not by the brand – the biggest reason the business is not currently sellable, since a buyer would be acquiring Adam, not a system. A values-first hiring brief does not exist, and George is the live example of skill hired before values.

## High-Control Register

The following touch money, customer ID, finance owing (PPSR), ownership, rego transfer or settlement paperwork, and are treated as high-control: how valuation inputs translate to a cash offer; the payment-to-settlement sequence, including when PPSR checks, ID verification and rego transfer happen; and any point where funds move before ownership is confirmed. None of this is assumed – each item is confirmed directly with Adam, precisely, before any SOP or automation is built around it.

## Strategic Hypotheses

1. Adam's "by feel" valuation logic can be broken into a testable rule set, letting a second person value a car within an acceptable margin.
2. A named, documented brand, separate from Adam personally, can carry the trust signal he currently carries alone.
3. The home-visit valuation, as a free entry tier, is the strongest lead mechanism for this audience.
4. Referrals and repeat-family transactions are under-systematised and are the fastest path to volume growth without added spend.
5. A values-first hiring brief, built from the Adam Hall Standard, is the precondition for breaking the key-person ceiling without repeating George.
