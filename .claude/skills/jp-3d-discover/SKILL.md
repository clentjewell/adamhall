# Jewell Projects 3D Process: Discover

The method for Phase 1. Discover turns raw client material into a validated, commercially sharp read that the whole engagement is built on. This skill holds the thinking and the quality bar. `jp-3d-core` holds the filing, codes, and gates. `jp-brand-document` holds the look and voice.

## Why this skill exists

The 3D template is a generic fill-in form by design. It supplies the structure and the prompts, not the judgement. A discovery output that only fills the template back to the client reads generic, because the template is generic until someone with method turns it into a specific commercial read. That conversion is the work. This skill encodes it.

If Discover goes generic, every downstream phase inherits it. Fixing the read here is the highest-leverage move in the whole process.

## The one rule: synthesise, do not recap

A recap repeats what the client said. A synthesis tells the client something they could not have said themselves: the real constraint, the white space, the commercial opportunity, the order of moves. Discover outputs synthesise.

### The non-generic test (apply to every paragraph)

Before any Discover paragraph is final, ask: **could this exact sentence have been written about any competitor in the category?** If yes, it is generic. Rewrite it with the client's own specifics, numbers, words, and proof. A Discover output that survives this test is doing its job.

## The ten signatures of a JP Discover output

These are the moves that separate a Pottsville-grade output from a template fill. Aim for all that apply.

1. **A one-sentence commercial read.** Name the real constraint. Pottsville: the problem is not credibility, it is commercial clarity. A stranger could not have guessed it.
2. **Confirmed versus pending, split cleanly.** State what is validated and what is still assumption. Never blur the two.
3. **White space as a quotable line.** One ownable sentence the client could put on a wall.
4. **Corrections captured as deltas.** When client review changes a fact, log the change (Pottsville: 35 years not 30, 62 reviews not 49).
5. **Offer built as tiers with a named spine.** Entry tier, commercial spine, LTV engine. Not a flat list.
6. **Bottlenecks named with their commercial consequence.** Not "weak follow-up" but "weak continuity of care is the biggest commercial bottleneck".
7. **Strategic hypotheses, not conclusions.** Discover proposes, strategy decides. Phrase as hypotheses to test.
8. **The client's own words preserved.** Capture verbatim phrasing for later voice work.
9. **Compliance threaded where the domain demands it.** Regulatory rules become constraints the downstream work obeys.
10. **Close on one owner, one action, one date,** and a feeds-into line stating what this hands off to.

## Working order

Build D01 through D05 as the evidence base, then D06 as the synthesis, then D06A if the domain needs it, then D07 and D08-DECK for the gate. D06 is the centrepiece. The earlier outputs exist to feed it. When asked only for "the discovery pack", that means D06, grounded in D01–D05.

Always read the inputs first (phase `01 Inputs`, `06 Source + Working + Imports`, `meetings`) and any client-specific deviations in KH00 before drafting. Capture the client's exact words as you go.

## D01 — Discovery Questions

**Input:** the 42-question master (brand essence, services, customers, brand personality, digital, social, KPIs, operations, life and legacy). Client answers, transcripts.
**Method:** tailor the master to the client's category, cut what does not apply, add category-specific questions, capture answers verbatim with attribution, flag gaps for follow-up.
**Quality bar:** questions provoke specific, quantified, outcome-focused answers, not yes/no. Answers are recorded in the client's own phrasing.
**Failure modes:** running the 42 unedited; paraphrasing answers and losing the voice; leaving quantified questions (sale value, LTV, lead split) unanswered.
**Pottsville anchor:** Dr Mal's own words for client goals ("function without pain, move more freely, increased energy, greater mental clarity") were captured at D01 and reused directly in the audience synthesis.

## D02 — Audience Teardown

**Input:** D01 answers, reviews, the client's read on who buys and why.
**Method:** run the teardown per segment (excruciating pain, purchasing power, reachability, growing demographic, riches in niches, market awareness, before to after transformation). Rank segments by commercial attractiveness. Name the lead growth pillar. Guard it from swallowing the whole identity.
**Quality bar:** every segment carries an excruciating pain stated in the customer's terms and a before-to-after. Segments are ranked, not equal.
**Failure modes:** listing demographics with no pain depth; treating all segments as equal weight; inventing personas with no input behind them.
**Pottsville anchor:** women's health named as the lead growth pillar at 50 percent investment, while men's health and injury recovery were flagged as active but underserved, a real commercial opportunity, not buried.

## D03 — Competitor Analysis

**Input:** named competitors, category scan, search visibility, the client's view of rivals.
**Method:** use the universal competitive template. Group competitors by type, not only by name. Choose two axes where the client can lead. Write contrast lines ("Unlike [type], [Client]..."). State the category gap and the category proof. Close with a one-sentence position.
**Quality bar:** identifies a specific, evidenced gap the client can own and a named benchmark to beat. The differentiation map is backed by proof, not drawn so the client wins by definition.
**Failure modes:** a generic SWOT with no battleground; a quadrant the client wins with no evidence; contrast lines that are claims, not truths.
**Pottsville anchor:** Athletica Health and Wellness named as the SEO benchmark, with a concrete battleground: symptom-based near-me searches the client was losing and could close.

## D04 — Killer Questions

**Input:** the unknowns whose answers would change the strategy (pricing logic, end-state definition, capacity, unit economics, channel reality).
**Method:** ask the few high-leverage questions, not many soft ones. Get them answered in writing by the principal. Convert each answer into a locked parameter the strategy will depend on.
**Quality bar:** answers are specific, quantified, and load-bearing. Each becomes an input the downstream work cannot ignore.
**Failure modes:** asking soft or numerous questions; accepting vague answers; failing to lock the answer as a parameter.
**Pottsville anchor:** KQ1 produced the three-tier pricing logic; KQ3 produced the sale-readiness definition in Dr Mal's own terms (3 practitioners, 66 clients per week, 7 replicable systems by end of 2027). Both became locked parameters.

## D05 — Offer Worksheet

**Input:** services, pricing, proof, guarantees, the transformation the offer delivers.
**Method:** structure the offer into tiers with a commercial spine. Map the path: entry tier, corrective spine, preventative LTV engine. State the re-education pathway that moves a buyer up the tiers. Write the one-line offer.
**Quality bar:** tiers are priced, the spine is named, the LTV engine is named, and the pathway between them is explicit.
**Failure modes:** a flat menu of services with no architecture; pricing with no tier logic; an offer line that describes rather than promises.
**Pottsville anchor:** Symptomatic Care as a valid entry point, Corrective Care as the commercial spine, Preventative as the LTV engine, with an explicit re-education pathway from quick-fix to ongoing care.

## D06 — Discovery Pack Synthesis (the centrepiece)

**Input:** D01–D05, transcripts, client review comments, prior versions.
**Method:** pull everything into one validated read. Lead with the commercial read, then evidence, then opportunity, then hypotheses, then actions. Separate confirmed from pending. State white space as a quotable line. Close on owner, action, date, and a feeds-into line.

Use this structure. It is the live Pottsville D06 shape, validated in production.

```
Front matter        engagement, phase, status, audience, date, supersedes, prepared by
Version updates     what changed since the last version, as deltas
Purpose of pack     what this does and what it is not (it is not the final strategy)
Summary on a page   business snapshot, core commercial read, immediate opportunity, key corrections
Executive summary   the sharp diagnosis: "the problem is not X, it is Y"
What we heard        the validated facts, in the client's terms
Business + model     services, pricing, operating model, capacity, sale-readiness target
Audience synthesis   primary and secondary segments, why they choose this client, channels
Brand direction      positioning, archetypes, pillars, voice, what to avoid (if confirmed)
Market + white space the ownable white-space sentence
Offer synthesis      the tier structure with named spine and LTV engine
Bottlenecks + risks  named, each with its commercial consequence
Strategic hypotheses to test in strategy, phrased as hypotheses
Production gates     what must be confirmed before production (if relevant)
Action items         a table: owner, action, due
Validated vs pending two clean lists
Footer               version, prepared by, feeds-into
```

**Quality bar:** every paragraph passes the non-generic test. The core commercial read is one sentence a stranger could not have guessed. Confirmed and pending are never blurred. The white space is quotable. Actions carry an owner and a date.
**Failure modes:** a recap of what the client said; no diagnosis; opportunity stated as a to-do list; confirmed and assumed mixed; no feeds-into line.
**Pottsville anchor:** the full annotated worked example is in `references/pottsville-d06-worked-example.md`. Read it before drafting a D06 for any client. It shows each signature in place.

## D06A — Discovery Addendum

**Input:** a domain constraint the standard pack cannot hold (regulatory, clinical, platform).
**Method:** create only when the client carries such a constraint. Capture it as rules the downstream work must obey, not as a disclaimer. Make each rule actionable.
**Quality bar:** rules are specific and enforceable, and named so design and copy can check against them.
**Pottsville anchor:** D06A held the AHPRA constraints as working rules: no guaranteed outcomes, no cure claims, no disease-name language. These governed all later copy and testimonials.

## D07 — Discovery Readout

**Input:** the final D06.
**Method:** convert D06 into a spoken narrative the client recognises. Lead with the commercial read. Confirm the corrections out loud. Surface the decisions you need from them.
**Quality bar:** the client says "yes, that is us" and can act on the decisions in the room.
**Failure modes:** reading the document aloud; burying the decisions.

## D08-DECK — Gate 1 Deck

**Input:** the final D06 and the readout.
**Method:** one idea per slide. Lead with the commercial read. Show confirmed versus pending. End on the decision and the path into Design. Build the deck with `jp-brand-presentation`.
**Quality bar:** a client could approve or redirect from the deck alone, without the document.
**Gate logistics:** filing and sign-off recording live in `jp-3d-core`. Record the sign-off in `00 Admin / 03 Approvals + Signoffs/`.

## Gate 1 readiness checklist

Discover is ready to close when:

- The commercial read is one sentence and passes the non-generic test.
- Confirmed and pending are split, and the pending list has owners and dates.
- The white space is stated as a quotable line.
- The offer is tiered with a named spine.
- Bottlenecks are named with their commercial consequence.
- Any domain constraint is captured as working rules (D06A).
- The synthesis closes with a feeds-into line to the Design phase.

If any of these is missing, Discover is not ready for Gate 1. Say so plainly.
