## Executive summary

Pottsville already has what most local clinics spend years building: trust, repeat loyalty, practitioner depth, a credible philosophy. The strongest read is that the growth problem is not clinical legitimacy, it is commercial clarity, plus a specific SEO visibility gap on symptom-based searches that competitors are filling. Today the value sits in the practitioner relationship. It is not yet translated into front-door positioning, treatment-tier structure, systematic nurture, founder-independent structure, or search visibility.

>> NOTE (signature 1): the "not X, it is Y" diagnosis is stated twice, sharply, and then itemised into the exact gaps. This is synthesis, not recap. It tells the client what is holding them back, not what they told us.

## What we heard and confirmed

35 years of experience with strong referral and repeat trust. Core services: acupuncture, TCM, cupping, moxibustion, gua sha, massage, lifestyle and dietary guidance. Differentiators: deep listening, practitioner depth, broader lifestyle guidance. Women's health is the lead growth pillar (50 percent) but not the whole identity. Men's health and injury recovery are active and underserved. The business is too dependent on Dr Mal; sale-readiness requires this to change by 2027. Booking via Halaxy. Word of mouth is primary; Google, Facebook, signage follow. Meta ads tested with zero conversion; community posts more effective. Athletica outranking on symptom-based near-me searches. Biggest bottleneck: continuity of care.

>> NOTE (signature 8): the client's own facts and phrasing are preserved. The lead-pillar-without-swallowing-identity nuance (signature 5 context) is captured exactly, not flattened.

## Business and operating model snapshot

Pricing confirmed: Symptomatic 1 to 3 sessions at about $100; Corrective 3 to 12 sessions ($492 for a 5 or 10-session pack); Preventative ongoing. Operating model: Dr Mal 12 appointments a day, 3 days a week, plus 2 practitioners; about 10 new clients a week to fill capacity. Sale-readiness target (KQ3): 3 practitioners, 66 clients a week, 50-week year, 7 replicable systems by end of 2027. Referral program: $50 gift card for GPs, pharmacists, gym practitioners.

>> NOTE (signature 5 and D04): the offer is already shaped as tiers, and the sale-readiness end state is quantified in the principal's own definition. These are locked parameters, not soft notes.

## Audience synthesis

Primary: local health-conscious adults, especially women, seeking holistic, preventative, root-cause care from a trusted practitioner. Client goals in Dr Mal's words: function without pain, move more freely, more energy, mental clarity, manage stress, live more courageously. Secondary: chronic pain and fatigue patients; men's health and injury recovery (underserved); long-term wellness clients; referral-led households. Why they choose Pottsville: trust, referral, 35 years, local reputation, integrative philosophy.

>> NOTE (signature 8): "in Dr Mal's words" then the verbatim list. This phrasing is reused later in voice and copy work. Capturing it here saves re-interviewing.

## Market and white space

White space: "Trusted local integrative care that treats the cause and the symptom, guided by 35 years of practitioner experience, and built around a supported path to recovery done together." The growth move is not inventing a new identity, it is making the existing depth easier to find, understand, trust, and buy.

>> NOTE (signature 3): the white space is a single quotable sentence. It is specific to this client and could not be lifted onto a competitor. It becomes the anchor for positioning.

## Offer synthesis

Three tiers, locked: Symptomatic (1 to 3 sessions, valid entry, including quick-fix clients); Corrective (3 to 12 sessions, the commercial spine); Preventative (ongoing, the LTV engine). Re-education pathway: guide quick-fix clients from Symptomatic to Corrective to Preventative. Continuity mechanisms to systematise: follow-up calls and emails, dietary guidance, programmes, Halaxy rebooking prompts.

>> NOTE (signature 5): entry, spine, and LTV engine are each named. The pathway between them is explicit. This is an architecture, not a price list.

## Core bottlenecks and risks

Founder dependence: a 35-year business still centred on Dr Mal. Weak continuity-of-care systems: patients not consistently guided through treatment blocks. SEO gap: symptom-based near-me searches won by competitors (Athletica benchmark). Under-packaged proposition: clinical depth stronger than front-door messaging. Sale-readiness timeline: 7 systems and 3 practitioners by end of 2027.

>> NOTE (signature 6): each risk is stated with its commercial consequence, not as a vague weakness. "Weak continuity of care" is named as the biggest commercial bottleneck, which directs the strategy.

## Strategic hypotheses

No brand reinvention first; a sharper commercial wrapper and better visibility. Fastest wins: positioning, tier framing, SEO content, nurture and rebooking, not aggressive acquisition first. Women's health leads but must not swallow the identity. Men's health and injury recovery are real underserved opportunities. Better continuity-of-care systems improve outcomes and performance. Pottsville is the proof engine and cash base; Meridiann is the future scale expression.

>> NOTE (signature 7): every line is a hypothesis the strategy phase will test, phrased as a proposition, not a decision already made.
